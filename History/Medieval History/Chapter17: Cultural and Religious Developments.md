## 1. Architecture: The Imperial Style

Mughal architecture blended **Indian** (Lintel, beam, brackets) and **Turko-Iranian** (Arch, Dome, Minaret) styles.

### A. Early Period (Babur & Humayun)

* **Babur:** Built formal **Gardens** (e.g., Aram Bagh in Agra) with running water.
* **Humayun:** His tomb (built by his widow) is a precursor to the Taj Mahal.
* **Features:** Massive marble dome, double dome (a device to build a bigger dome with a smaller one inside), Charbagh setting.



### B. Akbar's Era (Synthesis)

* **Forts:** Built massive forts in **Red Sandstone** at Agra, Lahore, and Allahabad.
* **Fatehpur Sikri:** A new capital city built on a hill.
* **Gujarat/Bengal Style:** Used deep eaves, balconies, and fanciful kiosks.
* **Panch Mahal:** Pillared structure resembling a Buddhist Vihara.
* **Buland Darwaza:** The "Lofty Gate" built to commemorate the Gujarat victory. Used a **Half-Dome Portal** style (sliced dome).



### C. The Climax (Shah Jahan)

* **Material Shift:** From Red Sandstone to **White Marble**.
* **Decoration:** Use of **Pietra Dura** (inlay work with semi-precious stones).
* **The Taj Mahal:** The jewel of builder’s art. Combined all elements: massive dome, four minarets, pietra dura, and gardens.
* **Mosques:** Moti Masjid (Agra - Marble) and Jama Masjid (Delhi - Red Sandstone).
* **Red Fort (Delhi):** New capital Shahjahanabad.

### D. Aurangzeb & Later

* **Decline:** Economy-mindedness led to fewer buildings.
* **Influence:** Mughal style influenced regional kingdoms (e.g., Golden Temple at Amritsar uses arch/dome principles).

## 2. Painting: A Living Tradition

### A. Foundation (Humayun & Akbar)

* **Origins:** Humayun brought two master painters from Iran (Mir Sayyid Ali and Abdus Samad).
* **Akbar's Workshop:** Established *Karkhanas* for painting.
* **Themes:** Court scenes, battles, hunting. Illustrated books like *Hamzanama* and *Mahabharata* (Persian trans.).
* **Synthesis:** Indian themes (peacock blue, red colors) blended with Persian styles. Introduced **3D effect** (shading/foreshortening). Famous painters: **Daswant** and **Basawan**.

### B. The Zenith (Jahangir)

* **Naturalism:** Shifted focus to portrait painting and **Nature** (animals, birds, flowers).
* **Mansur:** Famous for painting birds and animals.
* **Expertise:** Jahangir claimed he could identify individual artists in a composite painting.

### C. Later Period

* **Shah Jahan:** Continued the tradition but lacked Jahangir's passion.
* **Aurangzeb:** Disliked painting. Artists dispersed to **Rajasthan** (Rajput style) and **Punjab Hills** (Pahari style), leading to a revival in regional art.

## 3. Literature and Language

### A. Persian (Court Language)

* **Prose:** **Abul Fazl** (Akbar Nama) set a high standard of style.
* **Poetry:** **Faizi** (Akbar's poet laureate) and migrants like **Utbi** and **Naziri**.
* **Translations:** Massive department set up by Akbar.
* **Mahabharata** translated into Persian (*Razmnama*).
* **Ramayana**, **Atharva Veda**, **Bible**, and **Quran** translated.
* **Dara Shikoh** (Shah Jahan's son) translated the **Upanishads** and **Gita** into Persian (*Sirr-i-Akbar*).



### B. Hindi and Regional Languages

* **Brij Bhasha:** Patronized by Mughals. **Abdur Rahim Khan-i-Khanan** wrote Bhakti poetry blending Persian ideas.
* **Tulsidas:** Wrote **Ramcharitmanas** (Awadhi dialect). Emphasized devotion to Rama and a humanistic approach.
* **Marathi:** Eknath and Tukaram raised the status of Marathi.
* **Bengali:** Patronized by regional rulers (e.g., Alaol translated Persian works).

---

## 4. Music

* **Akbar:** Patronized **Tansen** (Gwalior). Fusion of Persian and Indian modes.
* **Aurangzeb:** Banned singing in court (personal austerity) but **not** instruments (he played the Veena).
* *Irony:* The largest number of books on classical Indian music in Persian were written during his reign.


* **Muhammad Shah (18th C):** Great patron; Music thrived later.

---

## 5. Religious Trends

### A. The Sikh Movement

* **Guru Nanak:** Preached unity of God and purity of character.
* **Guru Arjan Dev (5th Guru):** Compiled **Adi Granth**. executed by Jahangir (political reasons).
* **Guru Hargobind (6th Guru):** Militant turn. Clashed with Shah Jahan over hunting issues but generally not a major political threat yet.
* **Relations:** Generally peaceful until Aurangzeb's time.

### B. Liberal Trends (Bhakti/Sufi)

* **Dara Shikoh:** Called Vedas "Heavenly Books". Argued no fundamental difference between Hinduism and Islam (*Majma-ul-Bahrain*).
* **Dadu (Gujarat/Rajasthan):** Preached non-sectarian (*Nipak*) path.
* **Tukaram (Maharashtra):** Worship of Vithoba (Vishnu).

### C. Orthodox Reaction

* **Raghunandan (Bengal):** Rigidified Hindu Dharmashastras.
* **Shaikh Ahmad Sirhindi (Naqshbandi Sufi):**
* Opposed liberal policies (*Tauhid*).
* Demanded re-imposition of Jizyah and sternness towards Hindus.
* *Impact:* Limited during Jahangir/Shah Jahan's time but influenced later orthodox groups.



> **🌍 Current Context:**
> * **Composite Culture:** The Mughal era laid the foundations of the *"Ganga-Jamuni Tehzeeb"* (composite culture) visible in Indian food, dress (Sherwani/Kurta), music (Hindustani classical), and language (Urdu/Hindustani).
> * **Dara Shikoh:** Recently, the Indian government has renewed interest in finding the grave of Dara Shikoh, viewing him as a symbol of tolerance and a "what-if" figure in Indian history.
