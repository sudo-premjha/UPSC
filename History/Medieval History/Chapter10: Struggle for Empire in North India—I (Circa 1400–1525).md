## 1. Introduction: The Age of Regional Kingdoms (1400–1525)

**Context:** After Timur’s invasion (1398), the Tughlaq empire disintegrated.

* **The Vacuum:** Provincial governors declared independence (Gujarat, Malwa, Jaunpur).
* **The Balance of Power:** A complex rivalry emerged.
* West: Gujarat vs. Malwa vs. Mewar.
* East: Bengal vs. Orissa (Gajapatis) vs. Jaunpur.
* North: Lodis (Delhi) vs. Jaunpur.


* **Malwa as the Cockpit:** Dominating Malwa meant dominating North India. This rivalry eventually led Rana Sanga to invite Babur.

---

## 2. Eastern India: Bengal, Assam, Orissa

### A. Bengal

* **Independence:** Broke away from Delhi in 1338.
* **Ilyas Shah (Founder):**
* Unified Bengal. Raided Nepal and Orissa.
* Fought Firuz Tughlaq (Treaty fixed river Kosi as boundary).


* **Ghiyasuddin Azam Shah (1389–1409):**
* Famous for **Justice**.
* Re-established ties with **China** (Chittagong became a port for Chinese trade).


* **Alauddin Hussain Shah (1493–1519):**
* **Golden Age:** Brilliant ruler.
* **Liberal Policy:** High offices given to Hindus (e.g., Rupa and Sanatan Goswami). Respected **Chaitanya Mahaprabhu**.
* **Literature:** Patronized Bengali language. (Maladhar Basu wrote *Sri-Krishna-Vijaya*).



### B. Assam (Kamrup & Ahoms)

* **Two Kingdoms:** **Kamta** (West) and **Ahom** (East).
* **The Ahoms:** Mongoloid tribe from Burma (13th Century). Hinduized over time.
* **Suhungmung:** Greatest Ahom ruler. Took the title *Svarga Narayana*.
* **Vaishnavism:** Reformer **Shankaradeva** flourished here.

### C. Orissa (Gajapati Dynasty)

* **Rise:** After the decline of the Ganga dynasty (builders of Puri/Konark), the **Gajapatis** rose.
* **Power:** Extended rule to the South (Karnataka).
* **Frontier:** The river Saraswati was the boundary with Bengal.

## 3. Western India: Gujarat, Malwa, Mewar

### A. Gujarat

* **Founder:** **Ahmad Shah I** (1411–1443).
* Founded **Ahmedabad**.
* **Architecture:** Blended Jain traditions with Islamic style (slender turrets, stone carving). e.g., *Sidi Saiyyed Mosque*.
* **Religious Policy:** Mixed. Destroyed temples (Sidhpur) and imposed Jizyah, but employed Hindu ministers (Manik Chand).


* **Mahmud Begarha (1459–1511):**
* **Famous for:** Capturing two "Garhs" (Forts) - **Girnar** (Junagarh) and **Champaner**.
* **Personality:** Voracious appetite (ate honey/butter/bananas for breakfast).
* **Navy:** Tried to check the Portuguese (allied with Egypt) but failed.
* **Economy:** Trade flourished. Founded new towns (Mustafabad, Muhammadabad).



### B. Malwa

* **Strategic Location:** High plateau controlling routes between North and South India.
* **Capital:** **Mandu** (Famous for massive buildings like *Jahaz Mahal*, *Hindola Mahal*).
* **Rulers:**
* **Hushang Shah:** Tolerant policy (Jains were bankers).
* **Mahmud Khalji:** Restless/Ambitious. Fought everyone (Gujarat, Mewar, Delhi).



### C. Mewar (The Rajput Powerhouse)

* **Rana Kumbha (1433–1468):**
* **Achievements:** Defeated Malwa and Gujarat armies.
* **Builder:** Built **Kumbhalgarh** fort and **Victory Tower** (*Kirti Stambha*) at Chittor.
* **Scholar:** Wrote books on music and commentary on *Gita Govinda*.


* **Rana Sanga (1508–1528):**
* Grandson of Kumbha.
* Defeated Malwa ruler and Ibrahim Lodi (Battle of Khatoli).
* **Ambition:** To capture Delhi (invited Babur to weaken Lodis).

## 4. North India: The Sharqis & The Lodis

### A. The Sharqis of Jaunpur

* **Capital:** Jaunpur (Eastern UP). Known as **"Shiraz of the East"**.
* **Culture:** Great patrons of learning. **Malik Muhammad Jaisi** (author of *Padmavat*) lived here.
* **Architecture:** Unique style (Lofty gates/huge arches). E.g., *Atala Masjid*.
* **End:** Annexed by Bahlul Lodi (1484).

### B. The Lodi Dynasty (Delhi)

* **Origin:** **Afghans** (First Afghan dynasty in Delhi; previous ones were Turks).
* **Bahlul Lodi (1451–1489):** Founder. Defeated the Sharqis.
* **Sikandar Lodi (1489–1517):** Greatest Lodi ruler.
* **Agra:** Founded the city of **Agra (1504)** to control Rajasthan/Malwa.
* **Administration:** Efficient justice, cheap food grains. Introduced *Gazz-i-Sikandari* (measurement yard).
* **Religious Policy:** Bigoted. Destroyed temples (Nagarkot), re-imposed Jizyah.
* **Culture:** Patron of music (*Lahjat-i-Sikandar Shahi*).


* **Ibrahim Lodi:** Defeated by Rana Sanga and later by Babur (Panipat).

---

## 5. Kashmir (The Valley Kingdom)

### A. Background

* **Zulju (Dalucha):** Mongol invader (1320) devastated Kashmir, ending Hindu rule.
* **Sikandar Shah:** Known as *But-Shikan* (Idol breaker). Persecuted Brahmans.

### B. Zainul Abidin (1420–1470) - "Bud Shah" (Great Sultan)

* **Policy:** "Akbar of Kashmir". Reversed Sikandar's bigotry.
* Called back Hindus/Brahmans. Restored temples.
* Abolished Jizyah and cow slaughter.
* Allowed Sati.


* **Culture:** Well versed in Persian/Sanskrit. **Rajatarangini** (Kalhana) and Mahabharata translated into Persian.
* **Economy:** Introduced paper-making, book-binding, shawl-making, and musket-making.
* **Engineering:** Built **Zaina Lanka** (artificial island in Wular Lake).
