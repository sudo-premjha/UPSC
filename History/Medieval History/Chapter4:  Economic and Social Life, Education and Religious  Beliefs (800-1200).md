## 1. Economic Life (800–1200 AD)

**The Big Debate:** Historians debate whether this period was one of decline.

* **Arguments for Decline:** Scarcity of gold coins, decline of towns in North India.
* **Counter-Argument:** Trade actually flourished because new empires (Byzantine in West, Arabs in Middle East, Tang/Sung in China) created a massive demand for Indian goods.

### A. Trade and Commerce

**1. Foreign Trade (Flourishing):**

* **The West:** The rise of the Arab empire expanded trade. Gold/Silver flowed into India (India was a "sink" for precious metals).
* **Southeast Asia:** Known as *Suvarnadvipa*. Indian merchants settled there, intermarried, and spread culture (e.g., Angkor Wat in Cambodia, Borobudur in Java).
* **China:** The main focus of trade. Chinese consumed Indian spices, ivory, and gems.
* **Key Ports:**
* **Tamralipti (Bengal):** Gateway to SE Asia (Java/Sumatra).
* **Canton (China):** Had Hindu temples; Indian monks lived there.


* **Navigation:** Arabs and Chinese ships gradually overtook Indian ships because they used the **Mariner’s Compass** (a Chinese invention).

**2. Internal Trade (Stagnation):**

* **Decline of Guilds:** Due to the decline in long-distance internal trade, Guilds (*Shrenis*) lost power.
* **Caste Formation:** Old guilds transformed into sub-castes (e.g., *Dvadasashreni* became a Vaishya sub-caste).
* **Travel Bans:** Religious books banned travel beyond areas where "Munja grass" grows or across the "Salt Seas" (Kalivarjya). *However, merchants and craftsmen ignored these bans and travelled freely.*

> **UPSC Terminology - Guilds:**
> * **Manigramam & Nanadesi:** Famous South Indian trading guilds active in SE Asia.
> * **Vishti:** Forced labor that peasants had to render to the state/landlord.
> 
> 

### B. Agriculture & Feudalism

* **Expansion:** Kings gave land grants to Brahmans to settle in tribal/forest areas. This expanded cultivation but also created a class of "Landlords."
* **Indian Feudalism:**
* **Samantas (Feudal Lords):** Government officers and defeated chiefs were paid in **Land Grants** (Revenue assignments) instead of cash.
* **Hereditary Power:** These officials became hereditary. They collected revenue, dispensed justice, and maintained armies.
* **Burden on Peasant:** The peasant had to pay **1/6th revenue** + additional cesses (grazing tax, pond tax) + **Forced Labor (*Vishti*)**.

## 2. Social Life

### A. The Caste System (Jati)

The system became more rigid, but also more complex.

1. **Brahmans:** Position strengthened due to land grants. They monopolized education and religion.
2. **Rajputs:** A new warrior class emerged.
* **Origin:** Mixed origins (Foreigners like Hunas + Indigenous tribes).
* **Legitimacy:** Brahmans created the **Agnikula Legend** (born from fire) to give them Kshatriya status (e.g., Pratiharas, Chauhans, Solankis, Paramaras).


3. **Kayasthas:** A new caste of scribes/officials (originally people from various castes working in royal establishments).
4. **Shudras:** Position **improved** slightly. They were allowed some rituals and moved into agriculture (e.g., Jats moved from pastoralism to agriculture).
5. **Dalits (Antyaja/Untouchables):** Position **deteriorated**. Forced to live outside villages. Shadow considered polluting.

### B. Condition of Women

* **Status:** Considered mentally inferior; dependent on men.
* **Marriage:** Age lowered (6–8 years). Education denied (no Upanayana).
* **Property Rights (Improved):** To preserve family property, women were given the right to inherit. A widow could inherit her husband's estate if he died sonless.
* **Sati:** Increasing prevalence, especially among Rajputs (though some writers condemned it).
* **Exceptions:** Some queens ruled effectively, e.g., **Queen Didda** (Kashmir) and Prabhavati Gupta.

### C. Standard of Living

* **Elite:** Lived in luxury (imported silk/wine).
* **Commoners:** Poor. Often ate coarse grains/wild vegetables (*Utpala-saka*).
* **Dress:** Dhoti/Sari common. North India adopted trousers/jackets (Central Asian influence).
* **Food:** Vegetarianism growing, but wild meat (boar, peacock) was lawful. Wine was consumed on ceremonial occasions.

## 3. Education and Science

* **Nature:** No mass education. Technical training was family-based.
* **Universities:** **Nalanda** (Bihar), **Vikramasila**, **Uddandapur**.
* Supported by royal grants (Nalanda had 200 villages).
* Subject matter: Philosophy, Logic, Vedas.


* **Science (Stagnation):**
* **Surgery:** Declined because touching dead bodies was considered polluting (left to barbers).
* **Math:** **Bhaskara II** wrote ***Lilawati*** (Standard math text).
* **Medicine:** Use of minerals (mercury) began.
* **Al-Biruni’s Critique:** The Arab scholar Al-Biruni (11th Century) criticized Indian Brahmans for being haughty and refusing to share knowledge or learn from foreigners.

## 4. Religious Movements (The Great Transition)

This period saw the **Decline of Buddhism/Jainism** and the **Revival of Hinduism**.

### A. Decline of Heterodox Sects

* **Buddhism:**
* Confined to Eastern India (Palas).
* **Distortion:** Adopted **Tantra** (magical spells/mystic gestures). The **Gorakhnath/Nath-Panthi** movement rose (open to all castes).
* Became indistinguishable from Hinduism (Buddha became an avatar of Vishnu).


* **Jainism:**
* Popular in West (Gujarat - **Dilwara Temples**) and South (Karnataka - **Gomateswara Statue** at Sravana Belgola).
* Declined later due to loss of royal patronage.



### B. Revival of Hinduism

Hinduism became more inclusive but also established Brahmanical supremacy.

**1. North India:** Growth of **Tantra** (Magical rituals open to all castes).
**2. South India:** The **Bhakti Movement** (The most important development).

* **Nayanmars (Shiva) & Alvars (Vishnu):**
* Rejected austerities/caste.
* Preached love/devotion in local language (**Tamil**).
* Included lower castes and women (**Andal**).


* **Lingayats (Vir Saiva):**
* Founders: **Basava** and Channabasava (Karnataka).
* Worshipped Shiva.
* **Social Reform:** Opposed caste, child marriage; allowed widow remarriage.



### C. Philosophy (Vedanta)

To challenge Buddhism intellectually, great Acharyas reformulated Hindu thought.

| Philosopher | Philosophy | Core Belief |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Sankara** (9th C) | **Advaita** (Non-Dualism) | God and World are one. Knowledge (*Jnana*) is the path. World is *Maya* (Illusion). |
| **Ramanuja** (11th C) | **Vishishtadvaita** | Assimilated Bhakti with Vedas. Preached **Prapatti** (Total surrender to God) is open to all (including Shudras). |

> **🌍 Current Context:**
> * **Statue of Equality:** A massive statue of **Ramanuja** was inaugurated in Hyderabad (2022) to honor his contribution to social equality.
> * **Statue of Oneness:** A statue of **Adi Shankaracharya** was inaugurated in Omkareshwar, MP (2023).

### Summary for Revision

1. **Economy:** Foreign trade flourished (Arabs/China); Internal trade stagnated (Feudalism).
2. **Feudalism:** Rise of hereditary landlords (*Samantas*); burden on peasants (*Vishti*).
3. **Society:** Rise of **Rajputs** and **Kayasthas**. Women's rights mixed (property up, freedom down).
4. **Religion:** Buddhism declined. **Bhakti Movement** (Alvars/Nayanmars) and **Philosophy** (Sankara/Ramanuja) revived Hinduism.
