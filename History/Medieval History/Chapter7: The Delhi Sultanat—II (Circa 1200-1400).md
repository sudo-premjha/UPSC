## 1. The Khalji Revolution (1290–1320)

**Context:** After Balban's death, the Turkish nobility's monopoly on power was challenged. The **Khaljis** (mixed Turkish-Afghan origin) overthrew Balban’s successors. This event is called the "Khalji Revolution" because it ended the Turkish monopoly and opened high offices to non-Turks.

### A. Jalaluddin Khalji (1290–1296)

* **Policy:** Tolerance. He was the first Sultan to state that **India cannot be a truly Islamic state** because the majority population is Hindu.
* **Governance:** Tried to rule by the "willing support of the governed."
* **End:** Murdered by his nephew Alauddin Khalji.

### B. Alauddin Khalji (1296–1316)

He reversed Jalaluddin's tolerant policy and adopted "Blood and Iron" methods to control nobles.

**1. Consolidation of Power:**

* **Nobles:** Banned banquets/festivities/marriages among nobles without permission. Banned wine. Spy system established.
* **Mongols:** Massacred "New Muslims" (Mongols settled in Delhi) after a rebellion. Defeated severe Mongol invasions (1299, 1303).

**2. Expansion (The Imperial Phase):**

* **Gujarat (1299):** Sacked Anhilwara and Somnath. **Malik Kafur** (the slave general) was captured here.
* **Rajasthan:**
* **Ranthambhor (1301):** Defeated Hamirdeva. (First Persian description of **Jauhar** by Amir Khusrau).
* **Chittor (1303):** Defeated Ratan Singh. (Legend of **Padmini** is associated with this, though historians debate it).


* **Deccan & South (The Malik Kafur Expeditions):**
* **Objective:** Plunder wealth, not annexation.
* **Targets:** **Devagiri** (Yadavas), **Warangal** (Kakatiyas - Kohinoor diamond fame), **Dwar Samudra** (Hoysalas), **Madurai** (Pandyas).
* **Result:** Southern kings became feudatories and paid tribute.



**3. Reforms (Very Important for UPSC):**

* **Market Control Regulations:**
* **Objective:** To maintain a large standing army on low salaries (to fight Mongols).
* **Mechanism:** Fixed prices for all goods (grain to needles). Three markets set up in Delhi (Grain, Cloth, Horses/Slaves/Cattle).
* **Shahna-i-Mandi:** Officer in charge of the market.


* **Agrarian Reforms:**
* **Measurement:** First Sultan to insist on land measurement for revenue assessment.
* **Direct Collection:** State collected revenue directly (bypassing intermediaries like Khuts/Muqaddams).
* **Revenue Rate:** Raised to **50% (Half)** of the produce.

## 2. The Tughlaq Dynasty (1320–1412)

Founded by **Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq**. This dynasty saw the Sultanate reach its maximum territorial extent and then disintegrate.

### A. Muhammad Bin Tughlaq (1324–1351)

A highly educated "Rationalist" ruler, known for bold experiments that often failed due to poor implementation/timing.

**1. The 5 Ambitious Experiments:**

1. **Transfer of Capital (1327):** Delhi to **Daulatabad** (Deogir).
* *Reason:* To control South India better.
* *Result:* Failed. People suffered. Capital moved back. *Benefit:* Spread North Indian culture/Sufism to the South.


2. **Token Currency (1329):** Introduced Bronze coins with the same value as Silver Tankas.
* *Reason:* Global shortage of silver.
* *Failure:* People forged coins (every house became a mint). Had to be withdrawn.


3. **Khurasan Expedition:** Raised a huge army to conquer Central Asia. Disbanded it later (financial loss).
4. **Qarachil Expedition:** Sent army to Kumaon hills (Himalayas) to counter Chinese influence. Army perished in the cold.
5. **Doab Taxation:** Raised taxes in the Doab during a famine. Peasants rebelled.

**2. Agrarian Reforms:**

* **Diwan-i-Amir-i-Kohi:** Set up a separate agriculture department.
* **Takkavi Loans:** Given to farmers to buy seeds/cattle.
* **Crop Rotation:** Encouraged shifting from wheat to sugarcane/grapes.

**3. State of Empire:**

* **Rebellions:** The empire broke down. **Vijayanagara** (1336) and **Bahmani** (1347) kingdoms were founded in the South. Bengal became independent.

### B. Firuz Shah Tughlaq (1351–1388)

He inherited a disintegrating empire. His policy was **Appeasement** (of nobles, army, and theologians).

**1. Administrative Measures:**

* **Hereditary Principle:** Made Iqtas (land grants) and Army posts hereditary. This weakened the military (soldiers sent proxies).
* **Theologians:** Tried to rule according to Sharia to please the Ulema.
* **Jizyah:** Imposed it as a separate tax; **Brahmans** were taxed for the first time.
* **Intolerance:** Destroyed temples (Jagannath Puri, Kangra); persecuted heretical Muslim sects.



**2. Public Works (His Legacy):**

* **Canals:** Built the longest canal (Sutlej to Hansi) and Yamuna canal.
* **Cities:** Founded Hissar, Firuzabad, Jaunpur.
* **Humanitarian:** Set up **Diwan-i-Khairat** (Charity for marriage of poor girls) and **Dar-ul-Shifa** (Free hospitals).
* **Department of Slaves:** Collected 1,80,000 slaves (*Bandagan*). This later became a burden on the state.

## 3. Disintegration and Timur’s Invasion

**1. Decline:**
After Firuz, the Sultanate collapsed due to weak successors, powerful nobles, and the hereditary army system. The Sultan's rule shrank to *"Delhi to Palam"*.

**2. Timur’s Invasion (1398):**

* **Timur (Tamerlane):** Turk-Mongol conqueror from Central Asia.
* **Event:** Sacked Delhi mercilessly. Weak Tughlaq rulers fled.
* **Impact:** Death blow to the Tughlaq dynasty. Drained India’s wealth. Artisans taken to Samarkand.

### **Comparative Summary for Revision**

| Feature | Alauddin Khalji | Muhammad Bin Tughlaq | Firuz Shah Tughlaq |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Expansion** | High (South India tribute) | Peak (Direct control of South) | Low (Lost Bengal & South) |
| **Army** | Cash salaries, Branding horses (*Dagh*) | Huge standing army | Hereditary, Paid by land grants |
| **Religion** | Separated State from Religion | Rationalist (ignored Ulema) | Orthodox (Appeased Ulema) |
| **Reforms** | Market Control, 50% Revenue | Token Currency, Capital Shift | Canals, Slaves, Charity |
| **Nobility** | Suppressed/Controlled | Diverse (included low-borns) | Appeased (made hereditary) |

> **🌍 Current Context:**
> * **Minimum Support Price (MSP):** Alauddin’s market control is often cited as an early historical example of the state intervening to fix prices of essential commodities, similar to modern MSP debates.
> * **Water Management:** The canals built by Firuz Shah Tughlaq (Western Yamuna Canal) are still the lifelines of irrigation in Haryana and Western UP today.
