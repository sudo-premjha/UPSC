## 1. The Rise of the Marathas

**Background:** Marathas were initially *bargirs* (auxiliaries) for Deccani sultans.

* **Shahji Bhonsale:** Shivaji's father. Served Ahmadnagar and Bijapur. Tried to carve out a semi-independent principality.

### **A. Shivaji (1627-1680)**

* **Early Career:** Conquered hill forts near Poona (Raigarh, Kondana/Sinhagad) at age 18.
* **Conquest of Javli (1656):** A turning point. Defeated the **Mores** (Maratha chief) and mastered the Mavala highlands.
* **Conflict with Bijapur:**
* **Afzal Khan Incident (1659):** Bijapur general Afzal Khan tried to trap Shivaji. Shivaji killed him with tiger claws (*baghnakh*) and routed his army.


* **Conflict with Mughals:**
* **Shaista Khan (1663):** Mughal governor occupying Poona. Shivaji infiltrated his camp at night, wounded him, and killed his son. Shaista Khan was recalled in disgrace.
* **Sack of Surat (1664):** Looted the wealthy Mughal port.



### **B. Treaty of Purandar (1665)**

* **Jai Singh of Amber:** Deputed by Aurangzeb. He besieged Purandar and forced Shivaji to surrender.
* **Terms:**
1. Shivaji surrendered **23 forts** (kept only 12).
2. Accepted Mughal suzerainty (his son Sambhaji was given a rank of 5000).
3. Shivaji promised to help Mughals against Bijapur.


* **Visit to Agra (1666):** A disaster. Shivaji felt insulted at the rank given, was imprisoned, but escaped in a fruit basket. This ended any chance of a Mughal-Maratha alliance.

### **C. Coronation (1674)**

* Crowned himself at **Raigarh**. Assumed the title *Haindava-Dharmoddhara* (Protector of Hindu Faith).
* **Significance:** Legitimized his rule over other Maratha chiefs (*Deshmukhs*).
* **Karnataka Expedition (1676):** His last major campaign. Captured Jinji and Vellore.

### **D. Administration**

* **Ashtapradhan:** Council of 8 ministers. (Peshwa = PM; Senapati = Commander; Amatya = Finance).
* **Army:** Paid in cash. Strict discipline.
* **Revenue:**
* **Chauth:** 1/4th of revenue collected from neighboring (non-Maratha) lands for protection.
* **Sardeshmukhi:** Additional 10% claim as the head Deshmukh.

## 2. Aurangzeb and the Deccan (The "Deccan Ulcer")

Aurangzeb spent the last 25 years of his life (1682–1707) in the Deccan, which ultimately drained the empire.

### **Phase 1: Conquest (1681-1687)**

Aurangzeb moved to the Deccan to chase his rebel son **Akbar** (who joined Sambhaji). He realized Marathas couldn't be crushed without destroying their supporters: Bijapur and Golconda.

* **Annexation of Bijapur (1686):** Sikandar Adil Shah surrendered.
* **Annexation of Golconda (1687):** Abul Hasan Qutb Shah defeated.

### **Phase 2: War against Marathas (1687-1707)**

* **Execution of Sambhaji (1689):** Captured and executed. This was a **political blunder**. It turned the Maratha resistance into a people's war with no single head to negotiate with.
* **The Long War:**
* **Rajaram** (Shivaji's younger son) fought from **Jinji** (East Coast) for 8 years.
* **Tarabai** (Rajaram’s widow) continued the struggle after 1700.


* **Aurangzeb's Failure:** He captured forts only to lose them again. The Mughal army was demoralized.
* **Death:** Aurangzeb died in Ahmednagar (1707) with the war still raging.

## 3. Causes of Decline (The Analysis)

Why did the mighty Mughal Empire collapse?

### **A. Jagirdari Crisis**

* **The Problem:** Too many nobles (*Mansabdars*), too few good *Jagirs* (lands) to give them.
* **Be-jagiri:** Nobles waited years for a jagir.
* **Result:** Nobles squeezed the peasantry to get maximum revenue, leading to peasant revolts.

### **B. Agrarian Crisis**

* **Pressure:** Revenue demand was high (approx 50%).
* **Zamindars:** Powerful local chiefs often rebelled (Jats, Rajputs).
* **Peasants:** Fled lands or rebelled (Satnamis, Jats, Sikhs) due to oppression.

### **C. Political/Military Failures**

* **Maratha Failure:** Inability to integrate Marathas or crush them.
* **Rajput Policy:** Alienated Marwar (Rathores) and Mewar, losing loyal allies.
* **Military Stagnation:** Neglected modernization (unlike Europeans who had better infantry and naval power).

### **D. Aurangzeb's Responsibility**

* **Religious Policy:** Jizyah and temple destruction created disunity.
* **Deccan Policy:** Over-extended the empire. The "Deccan Ulcer" bled the treasury dry.
* **Suspicious Nature:** Did not trust his sons or nobles, leading to a lack of capable leadership after him.

> **🌍 Current Context:**
> * **Shivaji Maharaj:** Celebrated as a national icon and father of the Indian Navy (for his focus on sea power). His forts (Raigarh, Sindhudurg) are candidates for UNESCO World Heritage status.
> * **Asymmetric Warfare:** The Maratha tactic of *Ganimi Kava* (Guerrilla Warfare) against the massive Mughal army is a classic case study in military history of how a smaller force can tire out a superpower.
