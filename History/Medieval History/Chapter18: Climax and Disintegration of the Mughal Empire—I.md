## 1. The War of Succession (1657–1658)

The lack of a clear law of succession (Primogeniture was not followed) led to a bloody civil war among Shah Jahan's four sons.

### The Contenders

1. **Dara Shikoh:** Eldest and favorite. Liberal scholar (translated Upanishads). Viceroy of Punjab.
2. **Shuja:** Governor of Bengal.
3. **Aurangzeb:** Governor of Deccan. Able administrator and orthodox.
4. **Murad:** Governor of Gujarat.

### Key Battles

* **Battle of Dharmat (April 1658):** Aurangzeb and Murad defeated the Imperial army led by **Jaswant Singh** (Rathore ruler of Marwar).
* **Battle of Samugarh (May 1658):** The decisive battle. Aurangzeb defeated Dara.
* *Why Dara lost:* Poor generalship and tired troops. Aurangzeb had battle-hardened Deccan veterans.


* **Fate of Losers:**
* **Shah Jahan:** Imprisoned in Agra Fort for 8 years (nursed by Jahanara).
* **Dara:** Betrayed by an Afghan chief, captured, and executed on charges of heresy.
* **Murad:** Imprisoned and executed by Aurangzeb (breaking their alliance).
* **Shuja:** Driven into Arakan (Myanmar) and killed.

## 2. Aurangzeb’s Reign (1658–1707)

Aurangzeb ruled for 50 years. His reign saw the empire reach its maximum territorial extent but also face internal decay.

### A. Personality and Approach

* **Hardworking:** Strict disciplinarian.
* **Simple:** Disliked ostentation. Known as **Zinda Pir** (Living Saint).
* **Orthodox:** Wanted to rule according to *Sharia*.

### B. Religious Policy (The Controversial Aspect)

Aurangzeb reversed the liberal policy of Akbar, though recent research suggests political motives were as strong as religious ones.

**1. Measures Taken:**

* **Moral Policing:** Appointed **Muhtasibs** to enforce moral codes (check drinking, gambling).
* **Banned Practices:** Stopped *Kalma* on coins, *Nauroz* festival, *Jharoka Darshan* (showing himself to public), and weighing ceremony (*Tuladan*).
* **Music:** Banned singing in court (economy measure + orthodoxy), but instrumental music continued. *Irony:* Largest number of Persian works on Indian classical music were written during his reign.

**2. Temple Destruction:**

* **Initial Stance:** "Old temples should not be destroyed, but new ones not built."
* **Later Stance:** Destroyed temples as punishment for rebellions or if they were centers of "subversive ideas."
* **Famous Destructions:** **Vishwanath Temple** (Banaras), **Keshava Rai Temple** (Mathura), and temples in Jodhpur/Udaipur during the Rajput war.



**3. Jizyah (1679):**

* **Re-imposed Jizyah** (Poll tax on non-Muslims) after nearly 100 years.
* **Why?** To rally Muslim support against Maratha/Rajput uprisings and to bribe theologians (Ulama).
* **Impact:** Highly unpopular among Hindus; led to protests.

## 3. Political Developments in North India

### A. Expansion (North-East)

* **Assam (Ahoms):**
* **Mir Jumla:** Invaded Assam (1662), captured capital Garhgaon. Forced a humiliating treaty.
* **Reversal:** Ahoms adopted guerrilla warfare. Under **Lachit Borphukan**, they recovered lost areas (Battle of Saraighat). Mughals lost Guwahati.


* **Bengal:** **Shaista Khan** defeated the Arakanese pirates (Maghs) and captured **Chittagong** (1666).

### B. Popular Revolts

**1. Jats (Agra-Delhi Region):**

* Peasant uprising under **Gokla** (1669) and later **Rajaram/Churaman**.
* *Cause:* Agrarian grievances and religious resentment.
* *Result:* Led to the formation of the **Jat Kingdom of Bharatpur**.

**2. Satnamis (Narnaul):**

* A sect of artisans/peasants. Rebelled in 1672 but crushed.

**3. Afghans (North-West):**

* **Raushanai Movement:** Tribal uprising for independence.
* **Leaders:** Bhagu, Akmal Khan, and the poet-warrior **Khushhal Khan Khattak**.
* *Result:* Aurangzeb had to move to Peshawar personally to suppress them (1674).

**4. The Sikhs:**

* **Guru Tegh Bahadur (9th Guru):** executed in 1675.
* *Cause:* He supported Kashmiri Brahmans against persecution and was viewed as a threat to law and order by the state.


* **Guru Gobind Singh (10th Guru):**
* Founded the **Khalsa** (Military brotherhood) in **1699**.
* Fought battles against Hill Rajas and Mughals.
* His sons were executed by the Mughal governor of Sirhind (**Wazir Khan**).
* *Impact:* Transformed Sikhism into a militant resistance movement.

## 4. Relations with Rajputs (The Breach)

Akbar's alliance with Rajputs was strained under Aurangzeb.

### The Marwar Crisis (1678)

* **Event:** Death of **Jaswant Singh** (Ruler of Marwar) without an heir.
* **Aurangzeb's Move:** Brought Marwar under direct Mughal rule (*Khalisa*) pending succession. This was viewed as an act of aggression.
* **The Struggle:**
* **Ajit Singh:** Posthumous son of Jaswant Singh born in Lahore.
* **Durgadas Rathore:** The hero of Marwar. He rescued Ajit Singh from Agra and waged a 30-year war against Mughals.


* **Mewar Enters:** **Rana Raj Singh** of Mewar joined Marwar, fearing Mughal interference.
* **Rebellion of Prince Akbar:** Aurangzeb's son rebelled and joined the Rajputs (1681), posing a grave threat. He later fled to the Deccan (Sambhaji's court).

**Consequences:**

* The Mughal-Rajput alliance was weakened.
* Marwar remained in a state of rebellion for decades.
* Loss of prestige and military resources for the empire.

> **🌍 Current Context:**
> * **Lachit Borphukan:** The Ahom general who defeated the Mughals is celebrated as a national hero today. His 400th birth anniversary was recently commemorated by the Indian government.
> * **Gyanvapi Mosque:** The dispute over the Gyanvapi mosque in Varanasi stems from Aurangzeb's destruction of the Vishwanath temple, making his religious policy a topic of contemporary legal and political debate.
