## 1. Introduction: The Geopolitical Shift (1350–1565)

While North India faced disintegration after the Tughlaqs, South India witnessed stable empires for over 200 years: **Vijayanagara** and **Bahmani**.

* **End of an Era:** The Battle of **Bannihatti (Talikota)** in 1565 marked the end of Vijayanagara.
* **New Players:**
1. **Mughals:** Integration of North India.
2. **Portuguese:** Domination of the seas (First European naval power in India).



---

## 2. The Vijayanagara Empire (1336–1565)

### A. Foundation

* **Founders:** **Harihara and Bukka** (1336).
* **Legend:** Originally feudatories of Kakatiyas/Kampili. Captured by Muhammad Bin Tughlaq, converted to Islam, but reconverted to Hinduism by their guru **Vidyaranya** and founded Vijayanagara.
* **Expansion:** Defeated the Hoysalas (Mysore) and the Sultan of Madurai (by 1377).

### B. Conflict with Bahmani Kingdom

The two empires were constantly at war over three fertile/strategic areas:

1. **Tungabhadra Doab:** Land between Krishna and Tungabhadra rivers (wealthy/fertile).
2. **Krishna-Godavari Delta:** Fertile land + ports for foreign trade.
3. **Marathwada/Konkan:** Access to the port of **Goa** (vital for importing war horses from Iran/Iraq).

### C. Key Rulers & Events

**1. Deva Raya I (1404–1422):**

* **Conflict:** Defeated by Firuz Shah Bahmani; married his daughter to the Sultan (political marriage).
* **Construction:** Built a dam across Tungabhadra for irrigation and water supply to the capital.

**2. Deva Raya II (1425–1446):**

* **Greatest Ruler of Sangama Dynasty.**
* **Military Reform:** Realized Bahmani superiority lay in mounted archers. Recruited **2,000 Muslim cavalrymen** to train his Hindu soldiers in archery.
* **Travelers:**
* **Nicolo Conti (Italian, 1420):** Described the city's massive walls and power.
* **Abdur Razzaq (Persian):** Ambassador of Shah Rukh. Called Vijayanagara one of the most splendid cities in the world.



**3. Krishna Deva Raya (1509–1530):**

* **Dynasty:** Tuluva. Considered the greatest of all Vijayanagara rulers.
* **Military:** Defeated Bijapur (1520), Orissa, and recovered the Raichur Doab.
* **Literature:**
* Scholar in Telugu and Sanskrit. Wrote **Amuktamalyada** (Telugu polity) and *Jambavati Kalyanam* (Sanskrit drama).
* Patronized the **Ashtadiggajas** (8 Telugu poets).


* **Relations with Portuguese:** Used them to get horses and stop supplies to Bijapur. Allowed them to build a fort at Bhatkal.
* **Travelers:** **Domingo Paes** and **Barbosa** (Portuguese) praised his justice and the empire's prosperity.

### D. The Fall: Battle of Talikota (1565)

* **Rama Raja:** The de-facto ruler (Regent). He played off Muslim sultanates against each other.
* **The Alliance:** Bijapur, Ahmadnagar, Golconda, and Bidar united against Vijayanagara.
* **The Battle:** Fought at **Bannihatti (Talikota)**. Rama Raja was captured and executed. The city of Vijayanagara was sacked and ruined.

## 3. The Bahmani Kingdom (1347–1527)

### A. Foundation

* **Founder:** **Alauddin Hasan Bahman Shah** (Hasan Gangu).
* **Capital:** Gulbarga (later shifted to Bidar by Ahmad Shah I).

### B. Key Figures

**1. Firuz Shah Bahmani (1397–1422):**

* **Culture:** A scholar (botany, geometry, logic). Made the Deccan a cultural center.
* **Policy:** Inducted Hindus (Brahmans) into administration on a large scale.
* **Ports:** Developed Chaul and Dabhol.

**2. Mahmud Gawan (Prime Minister):**

* **Background:** Iranian trader who rose to become *Malik-ut-Tujjar* and then Peshwa.
* **Military:** Captured **Goa** and Dabhol (heavy blow to Vijayanagara). Used **gunpowder** effectively.
* **Reforms:**
* Divided kingdom into 8 provinces (*Tarafs*).
* Fixed salaries of nobles.
* Built a magnificent **Madarasa at Bidar**.


* **Death:** Executed in 1482 due to a conspiracy by Deccani nobles (Conflict between **Deccanis vs. Afaqis/Foreigners**).

### C. Disintegration

After Gawan's death, the kingdom broke into 5 Sultanates:

1. **Bijapur** (Adil Shahi) - *Most important*
2. **Golconda** (Qutb Shahi)
3. **Ahmadnagar** (Nizam Shahi)
4. **Bidar**
5. **Berar**

## 4. Administration and Economy

### A. Vijayanagara Administration

* **King:** Advised by a Council of Ministers.
* **Divisions:** Mandalam (Province)  Nadu (District)  Sthala  Grama.
* **Nayankara System (Important for UPSC):**
* The King granted territory (*Amaram*) to military chiefs called **Nayaks/Palegars**.
* They maintained troops for the king and paid a fixed sum.
* They became powerful and eventually independent (e.g., Nayaks of Madurai/Tanjore).


* **Village:** Self-government weakened compared to Cholas due to the rise of Nayaks.

### B. Economy

* **Agriculture:** Prosperous. Rice, cotton, indigo. Land tax varied (1/3 to 1/6).
* **Trade:** High urbanization. Temples participated in trade.
* **Gold:** Known for wealth. Gold coins (*Pagoda/Varaha*) were widely used.

## 5. The Portuguese in India

### A. Arrival and Objectives

* **Vasco da Gama:** Landed at Calicut (1498). Welcomed by Zamorin but opposed by Arab traders.
* **Goals:**
1. Oust Arabs/Venetians from the spice trade (Pepper).
2. Check the growing power of Ottoman Turks/Islam (Christian Missionary zeal).


* **Blue Water Policy:** **Almeida** (first Governor) focused on controlling the sea, not land.
* **Albuquerque (Real Founder):**
* Captured **Goa** (1510) from Bijapur.
* Strategy: Dominate trade by fortifying strategic ports (Goa, Malacca, Ormuz).
* Introduced the **Cartaz System** (Permits for ships).



### B. Impact

* **Trade:** Controlled the sea routes but couldn't monopolize Asian trade completely.
* **Crops:** Introduced **Tobacco, Potato, Maize, Pineapple, Cashew** to India.
* **Printing Press:** Introduced in Goa (mid-16th Century).
* **Science:** Did *not* act as a bridge for Renaissance science due to religious rigidity (Jesuits).

### C. Conflict with Ottomans

* The Ottoman Turks tried to break Portuguese dominance (Expeditions in 1538, 1551).
* **Result:** Failed. In 1566, they agreed to share the trade.

> **🌍 Current Context:**
> * **Hampi (Vijayanagara):** A UNESCO World Heritage site. The ruins of Hampi are a major tourist destination today, showcasing the architectural brilliance of the empire (Virupaksha Temple, Vittala Temple with musical pillars).
> * **Portuguese Civil Code:** Goa still follows a Uniform Civil Code, a legacy of Portuguese rule, which is a topic of current constitutional debate in India.
