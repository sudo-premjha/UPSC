# 1. The Rise of the Turks (Background)

**Context:** By the end of the 9th Century, the Abbasid Caliphate (Arab Empire) declined. The vacuum was filled by **Turks**.

* **Origin:** Palace guards and mercenaries from Central Asia.
* **Evolution:** Rose from guards to King-makers  Governors  Independent Rulers.
* **Titles:** Started as *Amirs*, later became *Sultans*.
* **Warfare Style:** Rapid advance/retreat, lightning raids, excellent horses.

### The "Ghazi" Spirit

* **Who were they?** Missionary-soldiers.
* **Motivation:** A mix of Religion (fighting "heathens") and Economics (plunder/salary).
* **Significance:** They acted as auxiliary forces that helped small Muslim states survive against non-Muslim tribes.

## 2. Phase I: Mahmud of Ghazni (The Raider)

**Time:** 998–1030 AD
**Goal:** Plunder wealth to finance Central Asian wars (Not to rule India).

### A. Rise of Ghazni

* **Alp-tigin:** A Turkish slave who founded the kingdom of Ghazni.
* **Mahmud:** Ascended the throne in 998 AD.
* **Dual Role:**
1. **Defender of Islam:** Against Central Asian tribes.
2. **Patron of Persian Renaissance:** Adopted Persian culture. **Firdausi** (poet laureate) wrote *Shah Namah* at his court.



### B. Conflict with India (The Hindushahis)

* **Region:** Peshawar and Punjab.
* **Rulers:** Jayapala and his son Anandpala.
* **The War:**
* **1001 AD:** Jayapala defeated near Peshawar.
* **1009 AD:** Anandpala defeated near Indus.
* **Result:** Punjab annexed to Ghazni. This breached India's North-West defense.



### C. The Raids (17 Expeditions)

Mahmud targeted wealthy temples and towns (the "banks" of that era).

1. **Kanauj (1018):** Sacked Mathura and Kanauj.
2. **Somnath (1025):**
* Most famous raid. marched via Multan/Jaisalmer.
* Destroyed the Shiva Lingam.
* Returned with immense wealth.
* *Note: No Rajput ruler came to aid the Shahis or effectively stopped Mahmud.*



> **🌍 Current Context - Bamiyan & Somnath:**

> * **Somnath Temple:** Was rebuilt several times. The current structure was reconstructed after India's independence (initiated by Sardar Patel) in 1951.

## 3. The Interregnum: The Rajput States

Between the decline of the Pratiharas and the arrival of Ghori, North India was ruled by Rajput clans.

### A. Major Dynasties

| Dynasty | Region | Key Ruler / Fact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Gahadavalas** | Kanauj | **Govind Chandra** (greatest ruler). Defended against Ghaznavids. |
| **Chauhans** | Ajmer/Delhi | **Vigraharaj** (Captured Delhi from Tomars). **Prithviraj III**. |
| **Paramaras** | Malwa | **Bhoja** (Writer on philosophy, yoga, medicine). |
| **Chandellas** | Bundelkhand | Built **Khajuraho** temples. |
| **Chalukyas** | Gujarat | Patronized Jainism (Dilwara temples). |

### B. Rajput Society

* **Structure:** Clan-based. Territory held by the Chief (*Rana*) and shared with blood-brothers (*Sub-chiefs*).
* **Strengths:** Sense of brotherhood, egalitarianism within the clan.
* **Weaknesses:**
* Constant feuds (War was treated as a sport).
* Exclusive nature (Non-Rajputs felt alienated from the ruling class).
* Lack of strategic vision (focused on internal land disputes, ignored the threat from Central Asia).



### C. Art & Architecture (Nagara Style)

This period (10th-12th Century) was the climax of temple building in North India.

* **Style:** **Nagara**.
* **Features:**
* **Shikhara:** Tall curved spiral roof over the *Garbhagriha* (Sanctum).
* **Mandapa:** Anteroom/Hall.
* **Pradakshina Path:** Circumambulatory path.


* **Examples:**
* **Khajuraho:** Kandariya Mahadeo temple (Chandellas).
* **Orissa:** Lingaraja Temple & Konark Sun Temple.
* **Dilwara (Mt. Abu):** Jain Temples (Vimal Vasahi).

## 4. Phase II: Muhammad Ghori (The Empire Builder)

**Time:** 1173–1206 AD
**Goal:** Conquest and Rule (Driven out of Central Asia by the Khwarizmi Empire, he *had* to expand into India).

### A. The Advance

1. **Multan/Uchch:** Conquered first.
2. **Gujarat (1178):** Tried to enter via desert. **Defeated** by the Gujarat ruler (Chalukyas) near Mt. Abu.
3. **Punjab (1190):** Changed strategy. Conquered Punjab (Ghaznavid possessions) to create a base for invading Delhi.

### B. The Conflict: Ghori vs. Prithviraj Chauhan

**1. First Battle of Tarain (1191):**

* **Winner:** Prithviraj Chauhan.
* **Event:** Ghori was wounded and routed. Prithviraj besieged Tabarhinda (Bhatinda) but did *not* chase Ghori out of Punjab (Strategic error).

**2. Second Battle of Tarain (1192):**

* **Winner:** Muhammad Ghori.
* **Event:** Ghori returned with better preparation (10,000 mounted archers). Prithviraj was captured and later executed.
* **Significance:** Laid the foundation of Turkish rule in India.
* *Note: Jaichandra of Kanauj stayed neutral (old rivalry).*

### C. Expansion (1192–1206)

1. **Battle of Chandawar (1194):** Ghori defeated **Jaichandra** (Gahadavala) and conquered Kanauj/Banaras.
2. **Bihar & Bengal:**
* Led by **Bakhtiyar Khalji**.
* Destroyed **Nalanda** and **Vikramasila** universities.
* Captured **Lakhnauti** (Sena capital) with just 18 horsemen (disguised as merchants).


3. **Assam Disaster:** Bakhtiyar Khalji tried to invade Assam/Tibet but was destroyed by the geography and Assamese resistance.

> **💀 The Death of Ghori:**
> He was killed in 1206 by a rebellion of the **Khokhars** (a warlike tribe in Punjab) and a Muslim fanatic on his way back to Ghazni.

## 5. Analytical Section: Why did the Rajputs Fail?

This is a favorite topic for UPSC Mains.

**1. Military Factors:**

* **Mobility:** Turks used swift horses and **Mounted Archers** (shoot while moving). Rajputs relied on slow elephants.
* **Tactics:** Turks fought as a compact unit. Rajputs fought as individual heroes.
* **Technology:** *Myth Buster* - Indians had the iron stirrup and good swords. The difference was organization, not weapons.

**2. Administrative/Social Factors (The Iqta vs. Feudalism):**

* **Turks (Iqta System):** Centralized. Commanders held land (*Iqta*) at the Sultan's pleasure. They maintained standing armies ready for the Sultan.
* **Rajputs (Feudalism):** Decentralized. Rulers depended on Chiefs. Chiefs maintained their own levies and often lacked coordination.

**3. Strategic Failure:**

* Rajputs lacked a "Strategic Vision". They never tried to reclaim the **Scientific Frontier** (Kabul/Hindukush) once it fell. They remained defensive behind the Indus.

**4. Social Exclusivity:**

* Rajputs formed exclusive groups. The common masses (non-Rajputs) did not feel a stake in the war ("War is the King's business").

### Summary of Key Terms for Prelims:

* **Amir-ul-Umra:** "Commander of Commanders" (Title given by Caliph).
* **Shah Namah:** Written by Firdausi (Persian Renaissance).
* **But-Shikan:** "Destroyer of Idols" (Title used by Mahmud).
* **Jahan-Soz:** "World Burner" (Title of Ghurid Sultan Alauddin for burning Ghazni).
* **Nagara:** North Indian temple style.
* **Iqta:** Revenue assignment in exchange for military service (Turkish system).
