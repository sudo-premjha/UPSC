## 1. The Political Landscape (Post-1565)

After the fall of Vijayanagara (1565), the three main Deccani states resumed their internal conflicts.

* **Ahmadnagar:** Wanted Berar and Sholapur.
* **Bijapur:** Wanted Sholapur and Naldurg.
* **Golconda:** Wanted to expand into the Vijayanagara territories.

### **The Rise of the Marathas**

* **Role:** Initially served as **Bargirs** (loose auxiliaries) in the Bahmani armies.
* **Evolution:** Rose to prominence as **Deshmukhs** (Zamindars) and military adventurers.
* **Policy:** Deccani rulers (like Ibrahim Adil Shah of Bijapur) actively courted them, employing them in revenue and military roles to strengthen local support.
* **Key Families:** Bhonsales, Ghorpades, Nimbalkars.

## 2. Mughal Advance into the Deccan

### **A. Akbar's Phase**

* **Motivation:**
1. **Strategic:** Secure the south after conquering Gujarat/Malwa.
2. **Sectarian:** Worried about the rise of **Shiism** (Golconda) and **Mahdawi** ideas in the Deccan.
3. **Portuguese Threat:** Wanted to control the coast to check Portuguese power.


* **Diplomacy (1591):** Sent embassies inviting Deccani states to accept suzerainty. All refused (except Khandesh).
* **War with Ahmadnagar (1595-1600):**
* **Chand Bibi:** The regent of Ahmadnagar who valiantly defended the fort against Prince Murad and Abdur Rahim Khan-i-Khanan.
* **Treaty (1596):** Berar ceded to Mughals.
* **Second Siege (1600):** Chand Bibi murdered by internal factions. Ahmadnagar fell.


* **Capture of Asirgarh (1601):** The strongest fort in the Deccan (Khandesh). This was Akbar's last conquest.

### **B. Jahangir's Phase: The Rise of Malik Ambar**

* **Malik Ambar:** An Abyssinian (Habshi) slave who rose to become the Peshwa (Prime Minister) of Ahmadnagar.
* **Tactics:**
* Revived the Ahmadnagar state by installing a puppet Sultan.
* Used **Guerilla Warfare** (*Bargi-giri*) effectively with Maratha help.
* Introduced **Todar Mal’s Land Revenue System** (Zabti) in the Deccan.


* **Conflict:** Defeated Mughal armies repeatedly. Recovered lost territories (Ahmednagar, Berar).
* **Jahangir's Response:** Sent Prince Khurram (Shah Jahan) who defeated the combined Deccani forces (1616 & 1618) but did not annex territory (Policy of Moderation).

### **C. Shah Jahan's Phase: The Extinction of Ahmadnagar**

Shah Jahan realized that peace was impossible as long as Ahmadnagar existed as an independent state.

1. **Isolation:** He bribed Bijapur (offered 1/3rd of Ahmadnagar territory) to join against Ahmadnagar.
2. **Defection:** **Shahji Bhonsale** (Shivaji's father) defected to the Mughals, then back to Ahmadnagar, and finally to Bijapur.
3. **Annexation (1633):** **Mahabat Khan** captured **Daulatabad**. The Nizam Shahi dynasty ended.

**The Settlement of 1636:**
A major diplomatic achievement by Shah Jahan.

* **Treaty with Bijapur & Golconda:**
* They accepted Mughal suzerainty.
* Agreed to pay annual tribute.
* **Golconda** came under Mughal protection.
* **Bijapur** was allowed to expand into the South (Karnataka).


* **Result:** This treaty ensured peace in the Deccan for 20 years, allowing the Deccani states to crush the southern Nayaks (Vijayanagara remnants).

## 3. Cultural Contribution of Deccani States

Despite constant wars, the Deccani states were centers of high culture.

* **Ali Adil Shah (Bijapur):** Known as a Sufi; possessed a great library.
* **Ibrahim Adil Shah II (Bijapur):**
* Title: *Jagat Guru* and *Abla Baba* (Friend of the Poor).
* Music: Composed **Kitab-i-Nauras** (Songs in praise of Hindu deities like Saraswati).
* Architecture: Built **Ibrahim Rauza**.


* **Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah (Golconda):**
* Founded **Hyderabad**. Built **Charminar** (1591).
* First poet to write in **Dakhini Urdu** (secular themes).


* **Architecture:**
* **Gol Gumbaz (Bijapur):** Largest single dome in the world (Whispering Gallery).
* **Charminar (Hyderabad):** Four lofty arches and minarets.
