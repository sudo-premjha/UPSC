## 1. Historical Background (Before the Cholas)

Before the Cholas rose to prominence in the 9th century, South India was dominated by three major powers between the 6th and 8th centuries:

* **Pallavas & Pandyas:** Dominated modern Tamil Nadu. Known for strong navies and trade with Southeast Asia.
* **Cheras:** Dominated modern Kerala.
* **Chalukyas:** Dominated Maharashtra/Deccan.
* *Key Fact:* The Chalukyan king **Pulakesin II** defeated Harsha, stopping his expansion into the south.



---

## 2. Rise of the Chola Empire (9th Century)

The Cholas brought the peninsula under one roof and turned the Bay of Bengal into a "Chola Lake" with their naval power.

* **Founder:** **Vijayalaya** (originally a feudatory of the Pallavas).
* He captured **Tanjore** in AD 850.


* **Expansion:** By the end of the 9th century, they defeated the Pallavas and weakened the Pandyas.
* **Setback:** They faced a temporary defeat against the **Rashtrakuta** king **Krishna III** (who annexed northern Chola territories), but they recovered rapidly after his death in 965 AD.

---

## 3. The Great Rulers: Rajaraja & Rajendra I

These father-son duos are the most important figures in Chola history. They expanded the empire from a regional kingdom to an imperial power.

### A. Rajaraja (985–1014 AD)

* **Naval Conquests:**
* Destroyed the Chera navy at Trivandrum.
* Conquered **Northern Sri Lanka**.
* Conquered the **Maldives**.


* **Motivation:** To control the trade routes to Southeast Asia (Coromandel and Malabar coasts were trade hubs).
* **Architecture:** Built the famous **Brihadishwara Temple** (Rajarajeswara temple) at Tanjore (completed 1010 AD).

### B. Rajendra I (1014–1044 AD)

* **Aggressive Expansion:**
* Completed the conquest of **Sri Lanka** (captured the King and Queen; Sri Lanka remained under Cholas for 50 years).


* **The "Ganga" Expedition (1022 AD):**
* Marched North, crossed the river Ganga, and defeated local kings in Bengal.
* **Significance:** Reversed the path of Samudragupta.
* **Title:** Assumed the title **Gangaikondachola** ("The Chola who conquered the Ganga").
* **New Capital:** Built **Gangaikondacholapuram** near the mouth of the Kaveri river.


* **The Southeast Asia Expedition:**
* Sent a naval expedition against the **Sri Vijaya Empire** (Malay peninsula, Sumatra, Java).
* **Reason:** The Sri Vijaya empire controlled the trade route to China; Cholas wanted to remove obstacles for Indian traders.
* **Result:** Conquered Kadaram (Kedah) and other places; temporarily controlled the Bay of Bengal.



> **🌏 UPSC Current Context:**
> The Chola naval expeditions are often cited today as early examples of India's maritime strategy and "Blue Economy" potential. The Indian Navy's "Project Mausam" aims to revive these ancient maritime links.

---

## 4. Trade and Foreign Relations

* **China:** Sent embassies in 1016 and 1033.
* A famous embassy of 70 merchants (1077 AD) exchanged articles like glassware, camphor, ivory, and rhinoceros horns for copper cash.


* **Conflict with Later Chalukyas:**
* Constant wars over Vengi (Rayalaseema) and the Tungabhadra doab.
* **Harsh Warfare:** Unlike the Dharmayuddha of earlier times, these wars were brutal. Cholas sacked cities (like Kalyani) and massacred civilians.



---

## 5. Chola Administration

The King was supreme, advised by a Council of Ministers.

### A. Military

* **Three Limbs:** Elephants, Cavalry, Infantry (mostly spearmen).
* **Bodyguards:** Sworn to defend the King with their lives (Marco Polo mentioned they would burn themselves on the King's funeral pyre).
* **Navy:** The strongest in the region at the time.

### B. Administrative Units

The empire was divided for better governance:

1. **Mandalam** (Province)
2. **Valanadu** (Group of Nadus)
3. **Nadu** (Basic unit - group of villages)
4. **Village** (Ur/Sabha)

* **Revenue:** Land survey was conducted. Revenue came from land tax, tolls on trade, and plunder from wars.

---

## 6. Local Self-Government (Village Autonomy)

**⚠️ Most Important Section for UPSC:** The Chola village system is the gold standard of ancient local governance.

There were two types of village assemblies:

| Assembly Type | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Ur** | The general assembly of a common village. |
| **Sabha (or Mahasabha)** | The assembly of **Agraharams** (Brahman villages with rent-free land). |

### How the Sabha Worked (The Committee System):

* **Executive Committee:** Managed village affairs.
* **Selection:**
* Members had to be educated and own property.
* Chosen by **drawing lots** or **rotation**.
* Retired every 3 years (preventing monopoly of power).


* **Specific Committees:** Separate committees existed for **Tanks** (water distribution), Garden, Justice, etc.
* **Powers:** Could collect taxes, raise loans, and own land.

> **🏛️ UPSC Current Context:**
> The **Uthiramerur Inscription** (Tamil Nadu) gives detailed accounts of this system. It is often quoted by the Prime Minister and historians to demonstrate that India has a long history of democracy (*"Mother of Democracy"* narrative).

## 7. Cultural Life: Architecture & Art

The Chola period is the **Climax of Dravida Style** architecture.

### A. Dravida Style Features

1. **Vimana:** The tall pyramidal tower rising above the sanctum (*Garbhagriha*). In Chola temples, these became very high (5-7 storeys).
2. **Mandap:** Pillared hall in front of the sanctum for audiences/ceremonial dances (*Devadasis*).
3. **Gopuram:** Lofty gates in the high walls enclosing the courtyard.
4. **Role of Temple:** Not just worship; it was a "miniature city"—a bank, a school, a landed owner, and a hub of social life.

### B. Famous Temples

* **Brihadishwara Temple (Tanjore):** Built by Rajaraja I. Massive vimana.
* **Gangaikondacholapuram:** Built by Rajendra I.
* **Hoysalesvara Temple (Halebid):** Built later by Hoysalas (Chalukyan style). Known for intricate sculptures of war, love, and music.

### C. Sculpture

* **Bronze Icons:** Reached zenith. The **Nataraja** (Dancing Shiva) is a masterpiece of this era.
* **Stone Sculpture:** Giant statue of **Gomateswara** (Bahubali) at Sravana Belgola (Jain influence).

## 8. Literature (The Growth of Regional Languages)

While Sanskrit remained the high language, regional languages flourished.

### A. Tamil

* **Golden Age:** The age of **Kamban** (11th-12th Century).
* **Kamban’s Ramayana:** A classic of Tamil literature.


* **Bhakti Literature:**
* **Nayanmars (Saivite):** Their works collected as *Tirumurai* (considered the Fifth Veda).
* **Alvars (Vaishnavite):** Flourished between 6th-9th centuries.



### B. Kannada

Patronized by Rashtrakutas, Chalukyas, and Hoysalas.

* **The Three Gems:** **Pampa, Ponna, Ranna**. (Jain influence, wrote on Ramayana/Mahabharata).

### C. Telugu

* **Nanniah:** Began translating the Mahabharata into Telugu at a Chalukyan court.
* **Tikkanna:** Completed it in the 13th Century.
