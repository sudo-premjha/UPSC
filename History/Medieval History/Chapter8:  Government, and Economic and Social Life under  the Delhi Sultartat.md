## 1. The Structure of Government

### A. The Sultan (The Centre of Power)

* **Status:** Supreme political, military, and legal authority.
* **Relation with Caliph:** Many Sultans called themselves "Lieutenant of the Faithful" and included the Caliph’s name in the *Khutba* (Friday prayers). This was a **moral position**, not a legal one, to legitimize their rule in the Islamic world.
* **Succession:** No clear law.
* Islamic theory suggested election, but in practice, **military strength** decided the successor.
* All sons had equal claims (Primogeniture was not strictly followed). Iltutmish even nominated a daughter (Raziya).



### B. Central Administration (The Departments)

This is a favorite area for UPSC Prelims matching questions.

| Department | Headed By | Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Diwan-i-Wizarat** | **Wazir** | **Finance & Revenue.** The Wazir was the key figure (Prime Minister). Under Tughlaqs, his influence peaked. |
| **Diwan-i-Arz** | **Ariz-i-Mamalik** | **Military.** Recruited, equipped, and paid the army. *Note: The Sultan was the Commander-in-Chief.* Balban set it up; Alauddin introduced *Dagh* (branding) and *Chehra* (descriptive roll). |
| **Diwan-i-Risalat** | **Chief Sadr** | **Religious Affairs.** Dealt with pious foundations, stipends to scholars. |
| **Diwan-i-Insha** | -- | **Correspondence.** Managed state correspondence with other rulers and officials. |
| **Diwan-i-Qaza** | **Chief Qazi** | **Justice.** Head of the judiciary (Civil law based on Sharia). |
| **Barids** | Chief Barid | **Intelligence/Spies.** Kept the Sultan informed. |
| **Household** | **Wakil-i-Dar** | Managed the royal household and *Karkhanas* (royal workshops). |

### C. Local Administration

The empire was divided into units for governance:

1. **Iqtas (Provinces):** Headed by **Muqtis** or **Walis**. They maintained law and order and collected revenue.
2. **Shiqs:** Sub-unit below the province.
3. **Pargana:** Group of villages (often 84, called *Chaurasi*). Headed by an **Amil**.
4. **Village:** The basic unit. Important officials: **Khut** (Landowner), **Muqaddam** (Headman), **Patwari** (Accountant).

## 2. Economic Life

### A. Agriculture

* **Crops:** Wheat, oilseeds (North); Rice, sugarcane (East/South). Two crops a year were standard. Indigo and cotton were cash crops.
* **Peasants:** Majority lived at subsistence level.
* **Rural Gentry:** Khuts and Muqaddams (Headmen) were prosperous. They rode horses and wore fine clothes until Alauddin Khalji curtailed their privileges.

### B. Trade and Urbanization

* **Urban Growth:** Delhi became the largest city in the eastern Islamic world. Daulatabad rivalled it.
* **Exports:** Textiles (Cotton/Muslin to China/West Asia), Indigo.
* **Imports:** High-grade textiles (Satin), Glassware, **Horses** (from West Asia), Raw Silk/Porcelain (from China).
* **Technology:**
* **Paper:** Introduced by Turks (reached Europe later).
* **Spinning Wheel (Charkha):** Improved textile production.
* **Rahat (Persian Wheel):** Improved irrigation (lifting water from deeper levels).
* **Mortar:** Enabled arch and dome architecture.



### C. Standard of Living

* **Nobles:** Extremely wealthy. Aped the Sultan's lifestyle.
* **Cost of Living:** Food was cheap (especially under Alauddin and Firuz). A soldier could live comfortably, but an unskilled laborer's life was hard.

## 3. Social Life

### A. The Caste System

* **Hindu Society:** Structure remained largely unchanged.
* **Brahmans:** Permitted to engage in agriculture in the "Kali Age."
* **Shudras:** Still restricted, but could hear Puranas (not Vedas). Severe restrictions on Chandalas (outcastes).


* **Muslim Society:** Also divided.
* **Racial Divide:** Turks, Iranians, Afghans, and Indian Muslims rarely intermarried.
* **Inequality:** Great disparity between the ruling elite and the common Muslim artisans/soldiers.



### B. Condition of Women

* **Hindu Women:** Early marriage continued. Widow remarriage prohibited for upper castes. **Sati** was prevalent (Ibn Battutah mentions needing Sultan's permission for it).
* **Property:** Widow's right to inherit husband's property (if sonless) improved.
* **Purdah (Veiling):** Became widespread in North India among upper classes (both Hindu and Muslim) due to Turkish influence and social prestige.

### C. Slaves

* **Prevalence:** Slavery was normal. Slaves were captured in war or imported (Africa/West Asia).
* **Markets:** Existed for men and women.
* **Firuz Tughlaq:** Collected **1,80,000 slaves**. Some were artisans, others bodyguards.

## 4. Nature of the State

### A. Theoretic Basis

* **Islamic or Secular?**
* Formally **Islamic** (followed Sharia).
* Practically **Secular (*Jahandari*)**: Sultans like Alauddin and Muhammad Tughlaq refused to let theologians (*Ulama*) dictate policy. They framed their own state laws (*Zawabit*).


* **Militaristic:** Dominated by a narrow clique of military leaders.

### B. Treatment of Hindus

* **Status:** **Zimmis** (Protected people). They paid **Jizyah** (tax in lieu of military service).
* **Jizyah:** Graduated tax. Women, children, and poor were exempt. **Brahmans** were exempt until Firuz Tughlaq taxed them.
* **Religious Freedom:**
* **War:** Temples destroyed (for plunder/justification).
* **Peace:** "Toleration within limits." Hindus practised religion openly. New temples were generally forbidden, but old ones could be repaired.
* **Barani’s Complaint:** He lamented that Hindus practiced idolatry publicly in Delhi and the Sultan could do nothing about it.



### C. Conversions

* **Not by Sword:** If force was the main method, the Delhi region would have been fully Muslim (which it wasn't).
* **Reasons:**
* **Political/Economic Gain:** Artisans (weavers, paper-makers) converted for patronage.
* **Tribal Conversion:** Tribes in Punjab/Bengal converted when they settled as agriculturists.
* **Sufis:** Their saintly character created a receptive climate, though they didn't actively seek mass conversion.



> **🌍 Current Context:**
> * **Jizyah:** Often debated in modern political discourse. It's important to remember it was a tax on able-bodied men in exchange for exemption from military service and protection of life/property.
> * **Technological Transfer:** The introduction of the **Spinning Wheel** and **Paper** by the Turks revolutionized communication and textiles in India, similar to how the internet changed modern commerce.
