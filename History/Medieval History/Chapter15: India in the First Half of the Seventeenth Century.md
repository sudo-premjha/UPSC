## 1. Political Developments (1605–1658)

### A. Jahangir (1605–1627)

* **Accession:** Succeeded Akbar without difficulty, though his eldest son **Khusrau** rebelled (defeated and imprisoned).
* **Consolidation:**
1. **Mewar:** Ended the 40-year conflict. Rana Amar Singh submitted on honorable terms (Prince Karan Singh was given a high rank).
2. **Bengal:** Subdued Afghan rebels (Usman Khan) and local rajas (Jessore/Kamrup) under the able governor **Islam Khan**.
3. **Deccan:** Brought Malik Ambar to heel (temporarily).


* **Nur Jahan:**
* Married Jahangir in 1611.
* Dominated the court (Title: *Badshah Begum*). Coins issued in her name.
* **The "Junta" Theory:** Some historians argue she formed a power group with her father (**Itimaduddaula**), brother (**Asaf Khan**), and Prince Khurram (Shah Jahan).
* **Impact:** Promoted Persian art/culture. Her rise caused friction with Shah Jahan and Mahabat Khan.


* **Rebellions:**
* **Shah Jahan (1622):** Rebelled due to fear of Nur Jahan's influence and refusal to go to Qandahar. Was defeated but later reconciled.
* **Mahabat Khan (1626):** Seized Jahangir in a coup. Nur Jahan’s clever diplomacy saved the Emperor.



### B. Shah Jahan (1628–1658)

* **Accession:** Ascended the throne with the help of his father-in-law, Asaf Khan.
* **Golden Age:** His reign is often called the Golden Age of Mughal architecture (Taj Mahal, Red Fort, Jama Masjid).
* **Expansion:**
* **Deccan:** Extinguished the **Ahmadnagar** kingdom (1633). Signed treaties with **Bijapur** and **Golconda** (1636), forcing them to accept Mughal suzerainty.
* **North West:** Captured **Qandahar** (1638) temporarily.

## 2. Foreign Policy (North-West Frontier)

The Mughals had to balance three powers: **Uzbeks** (Central Asia), **Safavids** (Iran), and **Ottomans** (Turkey).

**Core Objective:** Defence of India (Scientific Frontier: Hindukush mountains) and Trade.

### A. Relations with Uzbeks (Central Asia)

* **Akbar:** Established the Hindukush as the boundary.
* **Shah Jahan's Balkh Campaign (1646-47):**
* **Goal:** To secure a friendly ruler in Balkh/Badakhshan (buffer states) and control Afghan tribes. *Not* to conquer the Mughal homeland (Samarkand).
* **Event:** Prince Murad and later **Aurangzeb** led the army. Conquered Balkh but couldn't hold it due to hostile population and harsh winter.
* **Result:** Military success but political failure. Heavy loss of money/men.



### B. Relations with Safavids (Iran) and Qandahar

* **Importance of Qandahar:** Strategic (Defence of Kabul) and Economic (Trade route to West Asia).
* **Timeline:**
* **1595:** Akbar acquired Qandahar.
* **1622:** Persians captured it during Jahangir's reign (Shah Jahan refused to lead the relief army).
* **1638:** Shah Jahan recovered it (Ali Mardan Khan defected).
* **1649:** Persians recaptured it.


* **Failed Recovery:** Shah Jahan sent three major expeditions (under Aurangzeb and Dara Shikoh) to recover it but failed due to superior Persian artillery and the fort's strength. Qandahar was lost forever.

## 3. Administration (Evolution)

### A. Mansabdari System (Changes)

* **Du-Aspah Sih-Aspah:** Introduced by **Jahangir**. Allowed select nobles to maintain double the quota of troopers without raising their *Zat* rank.
* **Month-Scale System:** Introduced by **Shah Jahan**.
* Salaries were often paid for 10 months, 8 months, or even 5 months depending on the *Jagir's* actual income (*Hasil*) vs. assessed income (*Jama*).
* **Rule of 1/3rd or 1/4th:** Nobles were required to maintain only one-third or one-fourth of their *Sawar* rank quota.



### B. The Mughal Army

* **Cavalry:** The principal arm. Mixed contingents (Mughal, Pathan, Rajput) to prevent tribalism.
* **Ahadis:** Gentlemen-troopers. Directly recruited by the Emperor; highly paid.
* **Artillery:**
* **Heavy Guns:** For sieges (often clumsy).
* **Light Artillery:** "Artillery of the Stirrup" (highly mobile).


* **Weakness:** Lack of a strong **Navy** and drilled **Infantry** (compared to Europeans).

### C. Central Departments (Key Terms)

* **Wazir (Diwan):** Revenue head. (Position became less powerful than in the Sultanate period; checks and balances introduced).
* **Mir Bakhshi:** Head of Military (muster/branding) and Intelligence (*Barids*).
* **Mir Saman:** Head of Imperial Household and *Karkhanas*.
* **Sadr:** Religious endowments.

## 4. Economic and Social Life

### A. Economy

* **Trade:** flourished.
* **Exports:** Textiles (Cotton, Silk), Indigo, Saltpetre.
* **Imports:** Horses (from Iraq/Arabia), Glassware, Porcelain.


* **Urbanization:** Agra, Delhi, Lahore, and Ahmedabad were larger than London/Paris of that time.
* **Standard of Living:**
* **Nobles:** Lived in extreme luxury (*Karkhanas* supplied them).
* **Peasants:** Hard life but food was cheap. Famines were devastating.



### B. Society

* **Inequality:** Glaring disparity between the rich and poor.
* **Slavery:** Existed (Domestic/service oriented). Firuz Tughlaq had 1,80,000 slaves; Mughals continued the practice but on a smaller scale.
* **Women:** *Purdah* was common among upper classes. Sati existed (Akbar tried to restrict it).

> **🌍 Current Context:**
> * **The Taj Mahal:** Built by Shah Jahan for Mumtaz Mahal, it stands as the zenith of Mughal architecture and a symbol of India globally.
> * **Strategic Depth:** The Mughal struggle for the "Scientific Frontier" (Kabul-Qandahar line) is mirrored in modern geopolitical strategies regarding Afghanistan and the North-West frontier.
