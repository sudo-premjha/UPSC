# Cultural Interaction with Asian Countries

## 1. India's Relations with the Outside World

Despite medieval lawgivers ordaining that crossing the seas was taboo, India maintained intimate contacts with its Asian neighbors since ancient times.

* **Harappan Era:** Traders went to Mesopotamia (seals found there dating to 3rd millennium BC).
* **Historical Era:** Commercial contacts existed with China, Southeast Asia, West Asia, Central Asia, and the Roman Empire.

---

## 2. Buddhism: The Cultural Connector

### **A. Sri Lanka and Myanmar**

* **Sri Lanka:** Missionaries sent by **Ashoka** (3rd Century BC).
* **Legacy:** Sri Lanka became a stronghold where all Pali texts were compiled and commented upon.


* **Myanmar (Burma):** Buddhism spread here in the early Christian centuries.
* **Form:** Developed **Theravada** Buddhism.


* **Present Status:** While Buddhism disappeared from India, it remains the dominant religion in Sri Lanka and Myanmar today.

### **B. China**

* **Missionaries:** Started arriving from India during **Kanishka's** reign.
* **Exchange:** Chinese records mention **162 visits** by Chinese monks (5th–8th Century) to India.
* **Key Figures:**
* **Bodhidharma:** An Indian scholar recorded visiting China.
* **Pilgrims:** Fa-hsien and Hsuan Tsang came to India for texts.


* **Tun Huang:** A Buddhist colony and starting point for merchants crossing the desert.
* **Exchange of Knowledge:** Indians learned **Silk growing** from China; Chinese learned **Buddhist painting** from India.

### **C. Afghanistan and Central Asia**

* **Afghanistan:**
* **Begram:** Famous for ivory work similar to Kushan workmanship.
* **Bamiyan:** Boasted the **tallest Buddha statue** sculpted out of rock. Thousands of caves housed monks.
* **Decline:** Buddhism was supplanted by Islam in the 7th Century.


* **Central Asia:**
* **Language:** **Prakrit** written in **Kharoshthi** script spread here (many inscriptions found from 4th Century AD).
* **Usage:** Written language was used for official correspondence and religious propagation.



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Bamiyan Buddhas:** The text mentions the tallest rock-cut Buddha at Bamiyan. These 6th-century monumental statues were tragically destroyed by the Taliban in **2001**. However, the site remains a UNESCO World Heritage site, symbolizing the rich pre-Islamic Buddhist heritage of Afghanistan.

---

## 3. Christianity and West Asia

While trade with Rome declined, religious links persisted.

* **6th Century:** Alexandrian scholar **Kosmos** noted thriving Christian communities in India and Sri Lanka.
* **Organization:** A Bishop appointed from Persia served Christians in **Kalyan** (near Mumbai), coexisting with Jains, Buddhists, and Hindus.

---

## 4. Indian Culture in Southeast Asia

Culture here spread mostly through **Brahmanical cults** (unlike Buddhism in the North) and trade.

### **A. Key Regions**

* **Burma (Pegu/Moulmein):** Known as **Suvarnabhumi** (Land of Gold).
* **Java (Indonesia):** Known as **Suvarnadvipa** (Island of Gold).
* Earliest settlements: AD 56.
* **Sri Vijaya Kingdom (Sumatra):** Important center of Indian culture (5th–10th Century).


* **Indo-China (Vietnam/Cambodia/Laos):**
* **Kamboja (Cambodia):** Powerful kingdom founded in 6th Century. Rulers were devotees of **Shiva**; center of Sanskrit learning.
* **Champa (Vietnam):** Traders set up colonies. Official language was **Sanskrit**; center of Vedic education.



### **B. Art and Literature**

* **Borobudur (Java):** Largest Buddhist temple in the world (8th Century). Contains 436 images of Buddha.
* **Angkor Wat (Cambodia):** Larger than Borobudur. Walls feature reliefs from **Ramayana** and **Mahabharata**.
* **Ramayana:** Highly popular in Indonesia; many folk plays are based on it.
* **Language:** **Bahasa Indonesia** contains numerous Sanskrit words.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Living Heritage:** The text notes the popularity of the *Ramayana* in Indonesia. Today, the **Ramayana Ballet** performed at the Prambanan temple in Java is a world-famous cultural spectacle, performed largely by Muslim artists, showcasing the deep cultural syncretism of the region.

---

## 5. Cultural Give and Take

It was not a one-way imposition of culture but a "two-way traffic."

### **Fusion of Art**

* **Sculpture:** Buddha heads from Thailand/Kamboja and Bronze images from Java represent the best fusion of Indian and local art.
* **Painting:** Tun Huang caves (China) and Sri Lankan paintings compare to **Ajanta**.

### **Exchange of Goods and Technology**

| Acquired by India | Acquired by Neighbors |
| --- | --- |
| **Minting Gold Coins** (from Greeks/Romans) | **Growing Cotton** (from India) |
| **Growing Silk** (from China) | **Religion, Script, Art** (from India) |
| **Growing Betel Leaves** (from Indonesia) |  |

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Paan (Betel Leaf):** The text mentions Indians learned to grow betel leaves from Indonesia. Today, chewing *Paan* is considered a quintessential Indian habit, unaware of its Southeast Asian origins.
