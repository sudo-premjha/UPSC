### **Chapter 15: Territorial States and the Rise of Magadha**

#### **Conditions for the Rise of Large States**

From the 6th century BC onwards, large territorial states (*Mahajanapadas*) began to form in Eastern UP and Western Bihar.

* **Role of Iron:** Widespread use of iron weapons empowered the warrior class. Iron agricultural tools (ploughshares) increased food production.
* **Surplus & Taxation:** Surplus grain supported princes (military/administrative needs) and the new towns (5th C. BC).
* **Territorial Loyalty:** People began to owe allegiance to the *Janapada* (territory) rather than just the *Jana* (tribe).

---

#### **The Mahajanapadas (16 Great States)**

Buddhist texts (Pali) list **16 Mahajanapadas** existing in the age of the Buddha. They extended from the North-West Frontier to Bihar, mostly north of the Vindhyas.

**Key Mahajanapadas:**

* **Magadha (South Bihar):** Capitals **Rajgir** and later **Pataliputra**. Became the leading state.
* **Anga (East Bihar):** Capital **Champa**. Eventually swallowed by Magadha.
* **Vajji (North Bihar):** A confederacy of 8 clans. Capital **Vaishali** (Lichchhavi clan).
* **Kashi (Varanasi):** Initially powerful, later succumbed to Koshala.
* **Koshala (Eastern UP):** Capitals **Shravasti** and **Ayodhya**. Included the tribal republic of the Shakyas (Kapilavastu).
* **Mallas:** Republican clan (Kushinara - death place of Buddha).
* **Vatsa (Allahabad):** Capital **Kaushambi**. A Kuru clan settlement.
* **Avanti (Malwa/MP):** Capitals **Ujjain** (North) and **Mahishamati** (South). Serious rival to Magadha due to iron working.
* **Gandhara & Kamboja:** North-Western states. Kamboja (Tajikistan/Pamir area) is noted for excellent horses.

---

#### **Rise and Growth of the Magadhan Empire**

Magadha rose to power through the aggressive policies of three major dynasties.

**1. Haryanka Dynasty**

* **Bimbisara (544–492 BC):** Contemporary of Buddha.
* **Conquest:** Acquired Anga.
* **Matrimonial Alliances:** Married princesses of **Koshala** (brought Kashi village as dowry), **Vaishali** (Chellana - mother of Ajatashatru), and **Madra** (Punjab).
* **Diplomacy:** Sent physician **Jivaka** to cure Pradyota (King of Avanti) of jaundice.


* **Ajatashatru (492–460 BC):** Killed his father to seize the throne.
* **Wars:** Defeated Koshala (gained total control of Kashi) and Vaishali (Lichchhavis) after a 16-year war.
* **Innovations:** Used a **Catapult** (stone thrower) and **Chariot with Mace** (*Rathamusala*) to win wars. Fortified **Rajgir** against potential Avanti invasion.



**2. Shishunaga Dynasty**

* **Achievement:** Destroyed the power of **Avanti** (Ujjain), ending a 100-year rivalry. Avanti became part of the Magadhan empire.
* Temporarily moved capital to Vaishali.

**3. Nanda Dynasty**

* **Mahapadma Nanda:** Claimed to be *Ekarat* (sole sovereign). Conquered **Kalinga** (brought Jina image as trophy) and Koshala.
* **Military Strength:** Maintained a massive army (200,000 infantry, 60,000 cavalry, **3,000–6,000 war elephants**). Alexander dared not advance east due to this power.
* **Wealth:** Fabulously rich due to effective taxation.

---

#### **Causes of Magadha's Success**

Why did Magadha succeed where others failed?

1. **Iron Resources:** Richest iron deposits were near **Rajgir**, enabling superior weapons. (Avanti was the only real competitor due to its own iron mines).
2. **Strategic Capitals:**
* **Rajgir:** Impregnable, surrounded by 5 hills.
* **Pataliputra:** A "Water Fort" (*Jaladurga*) located at the confluence of the **Ganges, Gandak, and Son**. Commanded communications on all sides.


3. **Fertile Economy:** Situated in the fertile mid-Gangetic plains. Heavy rainfall allowed productive agriculture without irrigation, yielding surplus for taxes.
4. **Trade & Towns:** Rise of towns and metal money (punch-marked coins) allowed rulers to levy tolls and accumulate wealth.
5. **Elephants:** First to use elephants on a large scale in war (sourced from the east). Elephants could storm fortresses and cross marshy lands better than horses.
6. **Unorthodox Character:** Magadhan society was a mix of Kiratas and Vedic people. Being recently Vedicized, it had more enthusiasm for expansion than the orthodox older kingdoms.
