# Harsha and His Times

## 1. Harsha's Kingdom (AD 606–647)

After the decline of the Guptas (mid-6th Century), North India fragmented into smaller kingdoms and faced Huna invasions.

* **Rise of Thanesar:** The dynasty at **Thanesar** (Haryana) gradually extended authority over other feudatories. **Harshavardhana** united them and made **Kanauj** his seat of power.
* **Shift from Pataliputra to Kanauj:**
* **Pataliputra:** Declined because trade and money use fell.
* **Kanauj:** Rose because power shifted to military camps (*Skandhavaras*). Situated on an elevated area in the middle of the Doab, it was easily fortifiable and allowed control over both eastern and western wings of the river basin.


* **Extent of Empire:**
* **Direct Control:** Rajasthan, Punjab, UP, Bihar, Orissa.
* **Sphere of Influence:** Peripheral states acknowledged his sovereignty.


* **Rivals:**

![alt text](image-1.png)

* **East:** **Shashanka** of Gauda (Bengal), a Shaivite who cut down the Bodhi tree. (Hostility ended with Shashanka's death in AD 619).
* **South:** **Pulakeshin II** (Chalukya King of Badami) stopped Harsha’s southward march at the **Narmada River**.



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Kanauj:** The city of Kanauj (Kannauj), which rose to prominence under Harsha, is today famous as the "Perfume Capital of India," renowned for its traditional *Attar* (perfume) manufacturing.

## 2. Administration: The Feudal Transition

Harsha's reign marks the transition from ancient to medieval times, characterized by **decentralization**.

* **The Army:** Harsha reportedly had **100,000 horses** and **60,000 elephants** (much larger than the Mauryas). This was only possible because feudatories contributed quotas of soldiers, indicating a feudal structure.
* **Revenue Division:**
* **Part 1:** King’s expenditure.
* **Part 2:** Scholars.
* **Part 3:** Endowment of officials/public servants.
* **Part 4:** Religious purposes.


* **Land Grants:**
* **To Priests:** Continued for religious services.
* **To Officials:** Harsha began the feudal practice of paying ministers and high officers with **land grants** instead of cash. This explains the scarcity of coins from his reign.


* **Law and Order:**
* Not as well maintained as earlier times. The Chinese pilgrim **Hsuan Tsang** was robbed despite royal protection.
* **Punishment:** Robbery was "second treason"—punished by amputation of the right hand. However, under Buddhist influence, punishments were often mitigated to life imprisonment.

## 3. Hsuan Tsang’s Account (AD 629–645)

The Chinese pilgrim Hsuan Tsang (Xuanzang) spent 15 years in India (many at Harsha's court) and studied at Nalanda.

* **Social Life:**
* **Decline of Cities:** Pataliputra and Vaishali were in decline; Prayag and Kanauj were rising.
* **Differentiation:** Nobles/priests led luxurious lives; common Brahmanas/Kshatriyas led simple lives.
* **Shudras:** Described as **agriculturists** (a shift from earlier texts where they were servants).
* **Untouchables:** Scavengers/executioners lived outside villages, ate garlic/onion, and had to shout to announce their entry into towns so people could avoid them.

## 4. Buddhism and Nalanda

Buddhism was divided into 18 sects. The old centers were fading, but **Nalanda** flourished.

* **Nalanda University:**
* **Revenues:** Supported by the revenues of **100 villages** (later 200).
* **Monks:** Tradition claims 10,000 monks; Chinese pilgrim I-tsing (AD 670) observed 3,000.
* **Curriculum:** Focused on **Mahayana** philosophy.
* **Architecture:** Impressive complex raised/renovated over 700 years.



### **Harsha’s Religious Policy**

Originally a Shaiva, Harsha became a devout patron of Buddhism.

1. **Kanauj Assembly:**
* Convened to publicize **Mahayana** doctrines.
* Attended by 20 kings and thousands of priests.
* A huge tower with a golden Buddha was built.
* **Conflict:** Theological rivals tried to burn the tower and assassinate Harsha. Harsha executed/banished 500 Brahmanas in retaliation.


2. **Prayag Assembly:**
* Held every 5 years at the confluence of rivers.
* Harsha donated huge wealth, allegedly giving away everything except his personal clothing.



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Kumbh Mela:** Harsha’s great assembly at Prayag (Allahabad) to distribute charity is often cited as a historical precursor to the modern **Kumbh Mela**, which is still held at the same Triveni Sangam.

## 5. Literature

Harsha was a patron of learning and an author.

* **Banabhatta:** Court poet who wrote **Harshacharita** (Biography of Harsha). It set the model for ornate court poetry.
* **Harsha’s Plays:** Credited with writing three plays:
1. *Priyadarshika*
2. *Ratnavali*
3. *Nagananda*


* *Note:* Authorship is sometimes doubted (attributed to a ghostwriter named Dhavaka), as "royal authors are only half authors."
