### **Chapter 13: The Later Vedic Phase (c. 1000–500 BC)**

#### **Expansion and Texts**

* **Literary Sources:** The history of this period is based on the later Vedic texts compiled in the Upper Gangetic basin (c. 1000–500 BC):
* **Samhitas:** *Sama Veda* (tunes), *Yajur Veda* (rituals), *Atharva Veda* (charms/spells).
* **Brahmanas:** Explanations of rituals.


* **Archaeological Correlates:** **Painted Grey Ware (PGW)** sites (~700 sites found in Western UP, Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan).
* **Geographical Expansion:**
* Aryans expanded from Punjab to Western UP (Ganga-Yamuna *doab*).
* **Kuru Tribe:** Formed by merging **Bharatas** and **Purus**. Occupied Kurukshetra (Delhi/Upper Doab). Capital: **Hastinapur**.
* **Panchalas:** Occupied central doab (Bareilly, Badaun, Farukhabad). Famous for philosopher kings.
* **Later Expansion (c. 500 BC):** Spread east to **Koshala** (Eastern UP) and **Videha** (North Bihar). Encountered Munda speakers and users of Copper/Black-and-Red ware.



---

#### **Economy: Iron and Agriculture**

**Iron Technology:**

* **Global Context:** Pure iron first made in Mesopotamia (~5000 BC) and Anatolia (~3000 BC). Used as precious gifts until 1200 BC.
* **Indian Context:**
* **Early Use:** Pure iron found in Karnataka/Rajasthan (2nd half of 2nd millennium BC).
* **c. 1000 BC:** Used in Gandhara (graves), Baluchistan, Punjab, Western UP, MP, Rajasthan.
* **c. 800 BC:** Common use of iron weapons (arrowheads/spearheads) in Western UP helped defeat adversaries and clear forests.
* **Late Vedic:** Called *Shyama* or *Krishna Ayas* (Black metal).



**Agriculture:**

* **Status:** Became the **chief means of subsistence** (pastoralism became secondary).
* **Technique:** Primitive. Wooden ploughshares used (iron ones rare). Texts exaggerate 6–24 oxen per plough.
* **Crops:**
* **Rice (*Vrihi*)** and **Wheat** became chief crops. Rice found at Hastinapur/Atranjikhera (8th C. BC).
* Barley and lentils continued.


* **Labor:** Initially, even kings (e.g., Janaka) ploughed. Later, ploughing was prohibited for upper varnas.

---

#### **Material Life and Settlements**

* **Crafts:** Rise of smiths, smelters (iron/copper), weavers (women), leather workers, potters, carpenters, jewellers.
* **Pottery:** PGW (distinctive, used by upper class), Red Ware (most popular), Black-and-Red Ware.
* **Glass:** Hoards and bangles found.


* **Settlements (*Janapadas*):**
* Term *Janapada* (people's settlement) appears in Brahmanas (~800 BC).
* People lived in mud-brick/wattle-and-daub houses.
* **Proto-Urbanization:** Faint beginnings of towns (e.g., **Hastinapur**, **Kaushambi**) towards the end of the period.



---

#### **Political Organization**

* **Rise of Monarchy:** Royal power increased; tribal assemblies (*Sabha*, *Samiti*) lost power to chiefs/nobles. *Vidatha* disappeared. Women were excluded from *Sabha*.
* **Territorial Authority:** *Rashtra* (territory) and *Rajya* (sovereign power) concepts emerged. Tribes gave names to regions (e.g., Panchala).
* **Kingship:** Became hereditary (primogeniture).
* **Rituals of Power:**
* **Rajasuya:** Confers supreme power.
* **Ashvamedha:** Unquestioned control over territory (horse sacrifice).
* **Vajapeya:** Chariot race.


* **Administration:** *Sangrihitri* (tax collector) collected tributes (*Bali/Bhaga*). No standing army; tribal militia used.

---

#### **Social Organization (Varna System)**

* **Four Varnas:**
* **Brahmanas:** Priests. Grew powerful through the cult of sacrifice. Claimed exemption from harm.
* **Kshatriyas (Rajanyas):** Warrior nobles/rulers. Often in conflict with Brahmanas but cooperated to rule lower orders.
* **Vaishyas:** Commoners (agriculturists/traders). The **only tribute-paying class**.
* **Shudras:** Servants. "Servant of another," "to be beaten at will." Denied *Upanayana* (sacred thread) and Vedic education.


* **Gender:** Status of women declined. Denied *Upanayana*.
* **Gotra:** Institution of *Gotra* (cow pen -> lineage) appeared, enforcing exogamy.
* **Ashramas:** Four stages of life (Student, Householder, Hermit, Ascetic) conceptualized; only the first three clearly defined.

---

#### **Religion and Philosophy**

* **Gods:** Indra/Agni lost importance. **Prajapati** (Creator) became supreme. Rudra (animals) and Vishnu (preserver) rose in prominence.
* **Cult of Sacrifice:**
* Prayers replaced by elaborate sacrifices (*Yajnas*).
* Large-scale cattle slaughter (Guest = *Goghna*, fed on cattle).
* Priestly monopoly on formulae; huge *Dakshina* (cows, gold, horses) demanded.


* **Reaction (Upanishads c. 600 BC):**
* Compiled in **Panchala** and **Videha**.
* Criticized rituals; emphasized **Knowledge (*Jnana*)**, **Atman** (Self), and **Brahma** (Supreme Entity).
* Philosophy supported the stability required by the rising state power.
