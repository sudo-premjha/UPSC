# Life in the Gupta Age

## 1. System of Administration

The Gupta administration differed significantly from the Mauryas, characterized by decentralization and the beginnings of feudalism.

### **Kingship and Titles**

* **Titles:** Kings adopted pompous titles like *Maharajadhiraja* (King of Kings), *Parameshvara*, and *Paramabhattaraka*, signifying rule over lesser kings.
* **Succession:** Hereditary, but primogeniture (eldest son inheriting) was not strictly followed, creating instability.
* **Divinity:** Kings were compared to gods (Vishnu). Goddess Lakshmi appears on coins as Vishnu's wife.

### **Bureaucracy and Army**

* **Officials:** Less elaborate than the Mauryas. The most important officer was the *Kumaramatya*. Posts often became hereditary.
* **Army:** Standing army supplemented by feudatories. **Cavalry and Horse Archery** replaced chariots as the key military elements.
* **Revenue:** Land tax was **1/4 to 1/6** of produce. Villagers faced forced labor (*Vishti*) for the army/officials.

### **Administrative Divisions**

The empire was organized hierarchically:

| Unit | Official in Charge |
| --- | --- |
| **Bhukti** (Division) | *Uparika* |
| **Vishaya** (District) | *Vishayapati* |
| **Vithi** (Sub-division) | -- |
| **Grama** (Village) | Headman + Elders |

* **Urban Administration:** Professional bodies had significant power. In districts like Kotivarsha (Bengal), the board included the chief merchant, chief trader, and chief artisan.
* **Guilds:** Artisans and bankers had their own guilds with independent laws (proven by seals from Vaishali/Bhita).

### **Feudal Developments**

1. **Feudatories:** Vassals paid tribute, attended court, and offered daughters in marriage. They received charters with the Royal **Garuda Seal**.
2. **Land Grants:** Fiscal and administrative concessions were granted to priests/administrators. These lands were free from royal entry and tax, effectively creating independent pockets of authority.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Civil and Criminal Law:** The text notes that for the first time, civil and criminal laws were clearly demarcated during the Gupta period. This distinction remains the bedrock of the modern Indian legal system (Civil Procedure Code vs. Criminal Procedure Code).

## 2. Trends in Trade and Economy

### **Currency**

* **Gold Coins (*Dinaras*):** Issued in largest numbers during this period. Portrayed kings in war/artistic poses. Used for army pay and land sales.
* **Silver/Copper:** Silver coins issued for local exchange (especially after conquering Gujarat). Copper coins were very few (unlike Kushan times), suggesting money didn't reach the common people as much.

### **Decline in Trade**

* **Silk Trade:** Long-distance trade declined. Around **AD 550**, the Byzantines learned to grow silk from China, reducing demand for Indian silk.
* **Migration:** A guild of silk weavers migrated from Gujarat to Malwa in **AD 473** and abandoned their craft due to lack of demand.

### **Agrarian Expansion**

* Land grants to Brahmanas in tribal areas (Central India) brought virgin soil under cultivation and introduced advanced agricultural knowledge to tribal peasants.

## 3. Social Developments

### **Caste and Society**

* **Proliferation of Castes:** Due to:
1. Assimilation of foreigners (e.g., Hunas became Rajput clans).
2. Absorption of tribes into the Brahmanical fold via land grants.


* **Position of Shudras:** Improved. Allowed to listen to Epics/Puranas and worship Krishna. Transformed from slaves to agriculturists.
* **Untouchability:** Increased drastically. **Chandalas** lived outside villages; had to strike wood to announce their arrival (noted by **Fa-hsien**).

### **Position of Women**

* **Subordination:** Upper-caste women were treated as property.
* **Sati:** First inscriptional evidence of **Sati** (widow immolation) found in **AD 510**.
* **Property Rights:** While dependent on men, the scope of *Stridhana* (gifts to the bride) was enlarged; they could sell/mortgage it.
* **Remarriage:** Forbidden for upper castes but allowed for Shudras.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Stridhana:** The concept of *Stridhana* mentioned here is still recognized in modern Indian Family Law as the absolute property of a woman, distinct from dowry.

## 4. Religion

### **Bhagavatism (Vaishnavism)**

* **Origin:** Merger of Vedic Vishnu, cosmic Narayana, and historical Krishna-Vasudeva.
* **Key Tenets:** *Bhakti* (loving devotion) and *Ahimsa* (non-killing).
* **Avatars:** Doctrine of **Incarnation** (10 Avatars of Vishnu) evolved to save the social order.
* **Texts:** *Bhagavadgita*, *Vishnu Purana*, *Bhagavata Purana*.

### **Buddhism**

* Declined in royal patronage but still flourished in centers like **Nalanda** (founded 5th Century).
* Adopted features of Brahmanism (idol worship).

## 5. Art and Architecture

The Gupta period is the **"Golden Age"** of ancient India, particularly for the upper classes.

### **Sculpture and Painting**

* **Buddhist:** Life-size Copper Buddha (Sultanganj), Sarnath Buddhas.
* **Ajanta Paintings:** Most relate to this period. Depict Jataka tales; famous for brilliant colors that haven't faded.
* **Hindu:** Images of Vishnu and Shiva fashioned for the first time.

### **Architecture**

* **Temples:** Earliest brick temples survive from this period.
* **Bhitargaon** (Kanpur)
* **Deogarh** (Jhansi - Stone temple)
* **Nalanda University** (Earliest brick structures).



---

## 6. Literature

Sanskrit was the court language.

* **Secular Poetry/Drama:**
* **Kalidasa:** Greatest poet. Wrote *Abhijnanashakuntalam* (highly regarded globally).
* **Bhasa:** Wrote 13 plays.
* **Shudraka:** Wrote *Mrichchhakatika* (The Little Clay Cart).
* *Feature:* High-class characters speak Sanskrit; women/lower classes speak Prakrit (comedies).


* **Religious Literature:**
* **Epics:** *Ramayana* and *Mahabharata* were almost completed by the 4th Century AD.
* **Puranas:** Compiled in this era.
* **Smritis:** Law-books (e.g., *Narada Smriti*) compiled.


* **Lexicon:** *Amarakosha* by Amarasimha.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **The National Poet:** Kalidasa is often referred to as the Shakespeare of India. His work *Shakuntalam* remains a staple in Indian theatre and literature curriculums today.

---

## 7. Science and Technology

### **Mathematics and Astronomy**

* **Aryabhata (5th Century):** Wrote *Aryabhatiya*.
* Aware of the **Zero system** and **Decimal system**.
* (Inscription from Allahabad, AD 448, confirms knowledge of decimals).


* **Romaka Sidhanta:** Book on astronomy influenced by Greek/Roman ideas.

### **Metallurgy**

* **Iron Pillar (Mehrauli, Delhi):** Manufactured in 4th Century AD. Has not rusted for 15 centuries—a tribute to Gupta technology.
* **Bronze:** Advanced metal technology seen in Buddha images.
