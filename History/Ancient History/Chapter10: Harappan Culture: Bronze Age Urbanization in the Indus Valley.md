### **Chapter 10: Harappan Culture: Bronze Age Urbanization**

#### **Introduction**

The Harappan civilization, a Bronze Age urban culture, was a groundbreaking discovery.

* **Early Discoveries:**
* **1853:** **A. Cunningham** noticed a Harappan seal (bull + 6 letters) but missed its significance.
* **1921:** **Daya Ram Sahani** excavated Harappa (Punjab, Pakistan).
* **1922:** **R.D. Banerjee** excavated Mohenjo-daro (Sindh).


* **Geographical Extent:** Covered ~1.3 million sq. km (larger than ancient Egypt and Mesopotamia). It extended from Siwaliks (North) to Arabian Sea (South), and Makran Coast (West) to Meerut (East).
* **Major Sites:**
* **Harappa & Mohenjo-daro:** The two most important cities, linked by the Indus river.
* **Lothal (Gujarat):** Port city.
* **Kalibangan (Rajasthan):** "Black Bangles".
* **Dholavira (Gujarat):** Unique water management and stone architecture.
* **Rakhigarhi (Haryana):** Largest site (250 ha), larger than Mohenjo-daro (which was originally 500 ha but destroyed by floods).



---

#### **Town Planning and Structures**

* **Grid System:** Streets cut across one another at right angles.
* **Citadel vs. Lower Town:** Cities were divided into a Citadel (Western mound, for ruling class) and a Lower Town (Eastern mound, for common people).
* **Key Structures:**
* **Great Bath (Mohenjo-daro):** A masterpiece of brickwork used for ritual bathing. Dimensions: 11.88 x 7.01 x 2.43 m.
* **Granaries:** Found in Harappa (6 rows) and Mohenjo-daro (largest building). Used for storing tax grains.


* **Drainage System:** Unique to the Bronze Age. Drains covered with bricks/stone slabs connected every house to street drains. Attention to health/sanitation was unparalleled.

---

#### **Economy: Agriculture and Crafts**

* **Agriculture:**
* **Fertility:** Relied on annual Indus floods (similar to the Nile in Egypt).
* **Crops:** Wheat, Barley, Rai, Peas, Sesamum, Mustard. **Rice** found at Lothal (1800 BC). **Cotton** (Sindon) was a pioneering crop.
* **Tools:** Wooden ploughs (furrows found at Kalibangan), stone sickles.


* **Domestication:** Oxen, buffaloes, goats, sheep, pigs. Humped bulls were favored.
* **Horse:** Evidence is doubtful/superficial (Surkotada/Lothal). The culture was **not** horse-centered.


* **Crafts:**
* **Bronze:** Mixed tin with copper (arsenic occasionally used). Tin was scarce (imported from Afghanistan). Tools included axes, saws, knives.
* **Textiles:** Woven cotton and wool. Spindle whorls found.
* **Bead-making:** Experts in making beads from steatite, gold, and semi-precious stones.



---

#### **Trade and Commerce**

* **Nature:** Barter system (no metal money).
* **Transport:** Boats (coastal navigation) and bullock carts (solid wheels, no spokes).
* **Foreign Trade:**
* **Mesopotamia:** Records refer to trade with **Meluha** (Indus region) via intermediate stations **Dilmun** (Bahrain) and **Makan** (Makran coast).
* **Exports:** Lapis lazuli, beads, cotton, timber.



---

#### **Political and Social Organization**

* **Hierarchy:** Distinct residential structures (Citadel vs. Lower Town; 1-room vs. 12-room houses) suggest social stratification.
* **State Structure:** No temples found (unlike Mesopotamia/Egypt), suggesting rule by **merchants** rather than priests. Central authority is evidenced by uniform scripts, weights, and town planning.

---

#### **Religion**

* **Female Deity:** Terracotta figurines (e.g., plant growing from embryo) suggest worship of an **Earth/Fertility Goddess**.
* **Male Deity:** "Pashupati Mahadeva" seal – A three-faced yogi surrounded by animals (Elephant, Tiger, Rhino, Buffalo). Identification with Shiva is debated.
* **Nature Worship:** Pipal tree and animals (One-horned Unicorn, Humped Bull) were worshipped.
* **Phallus Worship:** Stones symbols of phallus (*linga*) and female sex organs (*yoni*) found.

---

#### **Script and Weights**

* **Script:** Pictographic, ~4000 specimens (mostly on seals). **Undeciphered**.
* **Weights:** Binary system (16, 64, 160...). The tradition of **16** continued in India (16 annas = 1 rupee) until recently.

---

#### **Decline (End of Indus Culture)**

* **Timeline:** Mature phase (2500–1900 BC). Decline by 1900 BC.
* **Causes:**
* **Environmental:** Shift of Yamuna/Sutlej away from Sarasvati (1700 BC) caused water scarcity. Decreased rainfall. Floods (Mohenjo-daro).
* **Economic:** Collapse of trade with Mesopotamia (rise of Elam ~2000 BC interrupted supply of tin/markets for beads).


* **Post-Urban Phase (1900–1200 BC):** Sub-Indus cultures (Chalcolithic nature) continued in Gujarat (Rangpur), Rajasthan (Ahar), and Punjab/Haryana. Writing and urban features disappeared.
