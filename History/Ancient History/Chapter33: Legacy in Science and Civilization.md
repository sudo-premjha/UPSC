# Legacy in Science and Civilization

## 1. Religion

Religion arose from man's confrontation with nature—both its bounty (fertility/rain) and its hazards (fire/flood).

* **Dominant Faiths:** **Brahmanism (Hinduism)** influenced art, literature, and society. **Jainism** and **Buddhism** also emerged.
* **Christianity:** Arrived in the **1st Century AD** but did not make much headway in ancient times.
* **Spread:**
* **Buddhism:** Disappeared from India but spread to Japan and Central Asia, projecting Indian art and language.
* **Jainism:** Continued in India, especially among trading communities in Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Karnataka.



---

## 2. The Varna System

Unlike other ancient societies where class duties were enforced by the state alone, in India, Varna laws had **both state and religious sanction**.

* **Structure:**
* **Brahmanas:** Priests.
* **Kshatriyas:** Rulers/Warriors.
* **Vaishyas:** Producers/Taxpayers.
* **Shudras:** Labourers.


* **Function:** It minimized class conflict. The lower orders worked hard believing in a better next life (Karma), reducing the need for the coercion seen in Greece or Rome (slavery/whip).
* **Legacy:** While it helped early economic growth through specialization, it eventually pitted castes against each other, preventing the oppressed from combining against the privileged.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Caste Politics:** The text notes that the caste system persists to this day. In modern India, while legally abolished, caste identity remains a powerful force in politics (vote banks) and social marriages, reflecting the deep roots described here.

## 3. Philosophical Systems

Indian philosophy is famous for spiritualism but also developed strong **materialistic** views.

* **Samkhya (Kapila, c. 580 BC):**
* **Materialist Origins:** Believed the world was created by **Nature (*Prakriti*)**, not God.
* **Knowledge:** Acquired through perception, inference, and hearing.


* **Nyaya (Logic):** *Nyaya Sutra* (c. AD 400) established logic as a discipline with four proofs (*Pramanas*).
* **Charvaka (Lokayata):**
* **Materialism:** Argued that what is not experienced by senses does not exist (implying gods do not exist).


* **Shift to Idealism:** With the decline of trade and urbanism, **Idealist** philosophy (Upanishads: "World is an illusion") came to the fore.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Schopenhauer and the Upanishads:** The text mentions the German philosopher Schopenhauer found solace in the Upanishads. This influence continues today, with Vedic philosophy influencing modern Western spirituality and thinkers.

---

## 4. Crafts and Technology

Indians made significant contributions to material culture.

* **Harappan Legacy:** Best town planning and fired bricks in the Bronze Age.
* **Dyeing:** Expertise in creating lustrous, lasting colors (e.g., **Ajanta paintings**).
* **Steel:**
* **Wootz Steel:** First developed in India (**200 BC**).
* **Export:** Indian steel swords were unmatched and in great demand from Asia to Eastern Europe.



---

## 5. Science and Mathematics

In ancient times, science was linked to religion (Astronomy for seasons/rituals; Grammar for Vedic prayers).

### **Mathematics**

* **Contributions:**
1. **Notation System:** Adopted by Arabs (who called numerals *hinds*) and spread to the West ("Arabic numerals").
2. **Decimal System:** Earliest epigraphic evidence in **5th Century AD**.
3. **Zero:** Discovered c. **2nd Century BC**. Arabs adopted it by AD 873.


* **Geometry:**
* **Sulvasutras (5th C BC):** Geometry for altar construction.
* **Apastamba (2nd C BC):** Practical geometry (acute/obtuse/right angles).


* **Trigonometry:** **Aryabhata** formulated methods to calculate the area of a triangle.

### **Astronomy**

* **Aryabhata (5th C AD):**
* Wrote *Aryabhatiya*.
* Calculated Earth's circumference correctly.
* Stated the **Sun is stationary and Earth rotates**.
* Explained solar/lunar eclipses scientifically.


* **Varahamihira (6th C AD):**
* Wrote *Brihatsamhita*.
* Stated the Moon rotates around Earth, and Earth around the Sun.
* **Applied Science:** His classifications of plants/animals and weather observations enriched agriculture.



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Computing:** The text highlights the Indian invention of **Zero** and the **Decimal System**. These are the foundational binaries of modern computing and digital technology used worldwide today.

## 6. Medicine (Ayurveda)

* **Origins:** *Atharva Veda* (initially magical charms).
* **Sushruta (2nd C AD):**
* Father of Surgery (*Sushrutasamhita*).
* Described operations for **cataract** and stone disease.
* Mentioned 121 surgical implements.


* **Charaka (2nd C AD):**
* *Charakasamhita* (Encyclopaedia of Medicine).
* Described fever, leprosy, tuberculosis.
* Listed numerous plants/herbs.



---

## 7. Art and Architecture

* **Ashokan Pillars:** Monolithic, polished pillars. The **Lion Capital** is now the National Emblem of India.
* **Ajanta Caves (2nd C BC – AD 7th C):**
* Birthplace of Asian art.
* Paintings display proficiency unmatched until the European Renaissance.


* **Gandhara Art:** Fusion of Indian and Hellenistic styles (first Buddha statues).
* **Temple Architecture:** South Indian temples influenced Southeast Asia (e.g., Angkor Wat, Borobudur).

---

## 8. Education: Nalanda

* **Nalanda University:**
* Residential-cum-teaching institution.
* Attracted students from Tibet and China.
* **Entrance Exam:** Only those passing the strict test by the *Dvarapandita* (Scholar at the Gate) were admitted.



---

## 9. Strength and Weakness

* **Strengths:** Staggering achievements in Harappan culture, science, steel making, and mathematics (Zero/Decimals).
* **Weaknesses:**
* **Social:** The caste system and the subordination of women (treated as property) persist as negative legacies.
* **Economic:** The practice of **Land Grants** (started by Satavahanas) led to unequal land distribution and impoverished cultivators—a problem visible even today.
