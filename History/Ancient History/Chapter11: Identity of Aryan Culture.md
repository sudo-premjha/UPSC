### **Chapter 11: Identity of Aryan Culture**

#### **Sources for Aryan Traits**

Our understanding of Aryan culture comes from a combination of ancient texts and linguistic analysis.

* **Key Texts:**
* **Rig Veda (c. 1500–1000 BC):** The oldest Vedic text.
* **Zend-Avesta (c. 1400 BC):** Ancient Iranian text.
* **Homer's Iliad & Odyssey (c. 900–800 BC):** Ancient Greek texts.


* **Shared Traits:** These texts reveal a culture that used copper/bronze (and later iron), practiced agriculture and pastoralism, lived in temperate climates, used horses and chariots, and had patriarchal societies.
* **Geographical Scope:** The cultural footprint covers Eastern Europe, Central Asia, and extends to India, Iran, and Greece.
* **Genetic Evidence:** The genetic marker **M17** links Indo-Aryan speakers. It is found in >40% of Central Asian steppe people and >35% of Hindi speakers in Delhi, supporting the migration theory.

---

#### **The Horse: Domestication and Significance**

The horse (*Asva*) is a defining feature of Aryan culture.

* **Textual Evidence:**
* **Rig Veda:** The term *asva* appears **215 times** (more than any other animal). Gods like Indra are associated with horses.
* **Avesta:** Many names of chiefs include *aspa* (horse). Prayers ask for swift horses.
* **Homer:** Horse and chariot are central to warfare and status.


* **Contrast with Tropics:** Tropical animals like the tiger and rhinoceros are absent in the Rig Veda (until later interaction), while the horse is central, pointing to a Central Asian origin.
* **Domestication Timeline:**
* **6000 BC:** South Ural / Black Sea region.
* **2000 BC:** Widespread use in Eurasia.
* **1700 BC:** Earliest true horse in South Asia (**Pirak complex**, Baluchistan).
* **Harappan Context:** Horse remains are scarce and doubtful (e.g., Surkotada). The culture was NOT horse-centered.



---

#### **The Chariot and Spoked Wheels**

* **The War Chariot:** Originated in West Asia (~4000 BC) and South Russia. Used by Mitanni rulers (~1400 BC) and described in Vedic/Greek texts.
* **Spoked Wheels:**
* **Innovation:** Lighter and faster than solid wheels.
* **Earliest Evidence:** **2300 BC** (Caucasus/Iran).
* **India:** Spoked wheels appear in the post-Harappan phase (e.g., Banawali). Mature Harappan toy carts have solid wheels.



---

#### **Cultural Traits and Rituals**

* **Pit-Dwelling:** Subterranean houses originated in cold climates (Ukraine/Central Asia). Found in India at **Burzahom** (Kashmir) and Swat Valley, indicating northern influence.
* **Birch (*Bhurja*):** A tree native to cold climates, used for construction in Central Asia and writing in India. Cognates exist in 6 Indo-European languages.
* **Cremation:** An Aryan trait supported by Vedic/Avestan texts. Contrast with Harappan earth burial. Earliest evidence in India is found in the Swat Valley (~1400 BC).
* **Fire Cult:** Central to Vedic (*Vedi*) and Iranian worship.
* **Animal Sacrifice:** Prevalent among all Indo-European communities (horse sacrifice, *Ashvamedha*, is particularly typical).
* **Soma Cult:** Drinking rituals involving *Soma* (Vedic) or *Haoma* (Avestan), likely identified with the **Ephedra** plant found in Turkmen temples.
* **Swastika:** Originated in Elam (~2000 BC) and adopted by Aryans later.

---

#### **Language and Origins**

* **Linguistics:** The most important attribute. Proto-Indo-European started c. 7000–6000 BC.
* **Inscriptional Evidence:**
* **2200 BC:** Earliest Aryan names (*Arisena, Somasena*) found in **Iraq** (Agade Dynasty).
* **19th–17th C. BC:** Hittite inscriptions (Anatolia).
* **16th–14th C. BC:** Kassite and Mitanni inscriptions (Mesopotamia).


* **Migration Direction:** No early Aryan inscriptions exist in India. Linguistic flow suggests migration from the West (Central Asia/Steppes) towards India, not vice versa.
