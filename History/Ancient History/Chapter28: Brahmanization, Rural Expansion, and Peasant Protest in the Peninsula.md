# Brahmanization, Rural Expansion, and Peasant Protest in the Peninsula (AD 300–750)

## 1. The New Phase (AD 300–750)

The period south of the Vindhyas from AD 300 to 750 marks the second historical phase.

* **Contrast with First Phase:**
* **First Phase (200 BC–AD 300):** Dominated by Satavahanas (Deccan) and Tamil Kingdoms (South). Characterized by thriving trade, coins, and towns.
* **Second Phase (AD 300–750):** Trade and towns declined. Rise of **land grants** to Brahmanas/temples, leading to massive agrarian expansion.


* **Political Fragmentation:** About two dozen states arose between AD 300–600 (known from land charters). By the 7th Century, three major powers emerged: **Pallavas** (Kanchi), **Chalukyas** (Badami), and **Pandyas** (Madurai).
* **Religion:** Shift from Megaliths/Buddhism/Jainism to **Triumphant Brahmanism** (Vedic sacrifices, stone temples for Shiva/Vishnu).
* **Language:** **Sanskrit** replaced Prakrit as the official language from AD 400 onwards.

---

## 2. States of the Deccan and South India

### **A. Vakatakas (Northern Maharashtra/Vidarbha)**

* Successors of Satavahanas.
* **Brahmanas** who championed Vedic sacrifices.
* **North India Link:** Culturally transmitted Brahmanical ideas to the South. Allied with **Chandragupta II** (via marriage to Prabhavati Gupta).

### **B. Chalukyas of Badami (Western Deccan)**

* **Capital:** Vatapi (Badami).
* **Origin:** Local Kanarese people who claimed high lineage (Ayodhya/Moon) to gain legitimacy.
* **Significance:** Dominant power in the Deccan until the rise of Vijayanagar. Overthrown by Rashtrakutas in AD 757.

### **C. Ikshvakus (Eastern Deccan - Krishna-Guntur)**

* Successors of Satavahanas in the east.
* Local tribe claiming Brahmana status and lineage from the mythical Ikshvakus.
* **Legacy:** Monuments at **Nagarjunakonda**. Started land grants in the region.

### **D. Pallavas (Kanchi)**

* **Name:** *Pallava* means creeper (Sanskrit version of Tamil *Tondai*).
* **Capital:** **Kanchi** (Kanchipuram), a center of temples and Vedic learning.
* **Authority:** Tondainadu (Southern Andhra + Northern Tamil Nadu).

### **E. Kadambas (Northern Karnataka/Konkan)**

* **Founder:** **Mayurasharman** (Brahmana).
* *Legend:* Insulted at Kanchi, he retreated to the forest, defeated the Pallavas, and established a kingdom.


* **Capital:** **Banavasi** (Vaijayanti).

### **F. Western Gangas (Southern Karnataka)**

* **Capital:** **Kolar** (Gold mines likely helped their rise).
* **Religion:** Made grants mostly to **Jainas**.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Kanchipuram:** The Pallava capital Kanchi is today known as the "City of Thousand Temples" and remains a major pilgrimage site and a hub for traditional silk weaving (*Kanjeevaram* sarees), reflecting its ancient heritage.

## 3. The Kalabhra Revolt (6th Century AD)

A unique event in South Indian history—a massive **peasant protest**.

* **The Kalabhras:** Tribal people (likely Buddhist) who captured power for 75 years, overthrowing Cholas, Pandyas, and Cheras.
* **Target:** Directed against **landed Brahmanas**. They revoked the *Brahmadeya* rights (tax-free land grants) given to priests.
* **Suppression:** It took a confederacy of Pandyas, Pallavas, and Chalukyas to crush them. The revolt signifies deep social unrest against the new unequal order created by land grants.

---

## 4. Conflict: Pallavas vs. Chalukyas

A long struggle for supremacy over the land between the **Krishna and Tungabhadra rivers** (the Doab).

* **Pulakeshin II (Chalukya, AD 609–642):**
* Most famous king (Aihole Inscription by **Ravikirti**).
* Defeated **Harsha** on the banks of the Narmada.
* Conquered Vengi (establishing the **Eastern Chalukyas**).


* **Narasimhavarman (Pallava, AD 630–668):**
* Occupied the Chalukya capital Vatapi (Badami) in AD 642.
* Title: **Vatapikonda** (Conqueror of Vatapi).


* **End:** Chalukya king **Vikramaditya II** routed the Pallavas in AD 740, but Chalukyas were soon overthrown by Rashtrakutas (AD 757).

---

## 5. Temples and Religion

The era saw the rise of **Bhakti** (Alvars/Nayanars) and temple architecture.

### **Pallava Architecture**

* **Mahabalipuram:** Seven Ratha temples (monolithic) and the **Shore Temple** (structural). Built by Narasimhavarman.
* **Kanchi:** **Kailashanath Temple** (8th Century).

### **Chalukya Architecture**

* **Aihole:** "Cradle of Indian Architecture" (70 temples).
* **Pattadakal:** 10 temples (7th/8th Century).
* **Papanatha Temple:** Northern (Nagara) style tower.
* **Virupaksha Temple:** Southern (Dravida) style tower.



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **UNESCO World Heritage:** The Group of Monuments at **Mahabalipuram** (Pallava) and **Pattadakal** (Chalukya) are both UNESCO World Heritage sites today, attracting tourists globally.

## 6. Agrarian Economy and Society

### **Demands on Peasantry**

* **Taxes:** Land tax, donations of cereals/gold.
* **Monopolies:** King controlled natural resources (salt, sugar, liquor from trees).
* **Forced Labour (*Vishti*):** Peasants had to provide free labor, bullocks, and provisions to royal officials/army.

### **Rural Expansion**

* **Land Grants:** Grants to Brahmanas in tribal areas spread advanced agriculture (new calendar, cattle preservation).
* **Village Types:**
1. **Ur:** Usual peasant village (land held in common).
2. **Sabha:** *Brahmadeya* village (owned by Brahmanas).
3. **Nagaram:** Village dominated by merchants/traders.



### **Social Structure**

* **Dominance:** Princes (claimed Kshatriya status via genealogies) and Priests (Brahmanas).
* **Caste in South:** Kshatriyas and Vaishyas were less important than in the North. Society was broadly divided into Brahmanas and non-Brahmanas (Peasant castes/Shudras).
* **Crisis (Kali Age):** Peasant refusal to pay taxes was termed *Kali Yuga* (Age of Evil). Kings took titles like *Dharma-Maharaja* to enforce order (i.e., suppress peasant dissent like the Kalabhras).
