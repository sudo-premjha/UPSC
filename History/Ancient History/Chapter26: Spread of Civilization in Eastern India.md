# Spread of Civilization in Eastern India

## 1. Signs of Civilization

A region is considered civilized when it possesses:

* Writing.
* System for collecting taxes and maintaining order.
* Social classes and specialists (priests, administrators).
* **Surplus Production:** Enough to sustain non-producers (consumers).

**Timeline:** These elements appeared late in Eastern India (Eastern MP, Orissa, West Bengal, Bangladesh, Assam). Virtually no written records exist prior to the **mid-4th Century AD**.
**The Shift (4th–7th Century AD):** This period is remarkable for the diffusion of advanced rural economy, state systems, and Sanskrit inscriptions (mostly land grants to Brahmanas/Temples) in these regions.

---

## 2. Orissa (Kalinga) and Eastern/Southern MP

### **Early History**

* **Kalinga (Coastal Orissa):** Rose to importance under Ashoka.
* **1st Century BC:** A strong state founded by **Kharavela** (advanced up to Magadha).
* **Trade:** Ports conducted brisk trade in pearls, ivory, and muslin. Excavations at **Shishupalgarh** (Kharavela's capital) yielded Roman objects.
* **Inland Lag:** North Orissa and inland areas remained backward without state formation.

### **State Formation (4th–6th Century AD)**

Samudragupta conquered parts of Orissa (Kosala/Mahakantara) in the 4th Century. Subsequently, five major states emerged:

1. **Matharas (Pitribhaktas):** Coastal area between Mahanadi and Krishna.
2. **Vasishthas:** South Kalinga (bordering Andhra).
3. **Nalas:** Forest area of Mahakantara (Tribal Bastar area).
4. **Manas:** Coastal area north of Mahanadi.

### **Cultural & Economic Developments**

* **Currency:** Nala gold coins (Bastar) and Mana copper coins indicate a money economy even in tribal areas.
* **New Fiscal Units:** Matharas created districts like *Dantayavagubhoga* (supplied ivory and rice-gruel).
* **Agraharas:** Land grants to Brahmanas in tribal/forest areas brought new land under cultivation and introduced advanced agriculture (e.g., dividing the year into **12 lunar months** instead of 3 seasons).
* **Sanskritization:** Sanskrit replaced Prakrit after AD 350. It became the vehicle for property laws and social regulations, linking Orissa to the culture of the Gangetic basin.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **The Lunar Calendar:** The text mentions that the practice of dividing the year into 12 lunar months was introduced in Orissa during the Mathara rule (5th Century). This lunar calendar system remains the basis for all Hindu religious festivals (like Rath Yatra) in Odisha today.

## 3. Bengal

### **Early Traces**

* **Ashokan Era:** Evidence of writing in North Bengal (Bogra district) and storehouses for Buddhist monks.
* **2nd Century BC:** Prakrit/Brahmi known in Noakhali (South-East Bengal).
* **Gap:** Silence until the 4th Century AD.

### **The Formative Phase (4th–7th Century AD)**

1. **North Bengal (Pundravardhana):**
* **Land Sale Documents:** Copperplates (AD 432–550) record land sales using **Dinara** (gold coins).
* **Administration:** Gupta governors administered the area. Local administration included scribes, merchants, and artisans.
* **Land Reclamation:** Waste/fallow land was granted to Brahmanas/Temples, expanding cultivation.
* **Gauda:** By AD 600, this area became the independent state of **Gauda** under **Shashanka** (Harsha’s adversary).


2. **South-East Bengal (Samatata/Vanga):**
* **Samatata:** Acknowledged Samudragupta's authority (4th Century).
* **Kingdoms:** By AD 525, organized states emerged (e.g., Khadgas in Dhaka, Ratas in Comilla).
* **Expansion:** Fiscal units like *Dandabhukti* (Burdwan border) were created to suppress tribal inhabitants. Land grants turned forest regions (full of tigers/buffaloes) into settlements.



### **Society and Economy**

* **Religion:** 5th Century saw the setting up of Buddha statues (rare earlier). Monasteries established.
* **Land Rights:** Originally, land alienation required community consent. Over time, kings arrogated more rights to make grants.
* **Language:** Sanskrit was the official language known by administrative functionaries.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **"Gauda" and "Vanga":** The ancient names mentioned here still persist. "Vanga" is the root for **"Banga"** (Bengal) and **"Bangladesh"**. "Gauda" is still used in cultural contexts (e.g., Gaudiya Vaishnavism).

## 4. Assam (Kamarupa)

* **4th Century AD:** **Kamarupa** (Brahmaputra basin) and **Davaka** (Nowgong) paid tribute to Samudragupta.
* **Ambari Excavations:** Show developed settlements near Guwahati by the 6th–7th Century.
* **Sanskritization:** Kings adopted the title **Varman** (Armour/Kshatriya title) and made land grants to Brahmanas.
* **Bhaskaravarman (7th Century):** Emerged as a powerful head of state. Visited by Chinese pilgrim **Hsuan Tsang**.

---

## 5. The Formative Phase (Summary)

The period **4th–7th Century AD** was the turning point for Eastern India.

* **Diffusion:** Writing, Sanskrit, Vedic rituals, and the Varna system spread to MP, Orissa, Bengal, and Assam.
* **State Formation:** Feudatories formed new states, maintained professional armies (elephants/boats), and collected taxes.
* **Economy:** Advanced food-producing economy based on **Iron Ploughshare**, **Wet Paddy Cultivation**, and **Sugarcane**.
* **Social Change:** Tribal areas (red soil/jungle regions) were assimilated into the Brahmanical order through land grants (*Agraharas*).
* **Coinage:** Use of gold coins for land purchase indicates commercial advancement.
