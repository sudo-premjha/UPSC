### **Chapter 9: Chalcolithic Cultures**

#### **Chalcolithic Settlements**

The end of the Neolithic period marked the transition to the **Chalcolithic Age** (Copper-Stone Age), characterized by the use of **copper** and stone implements.

* **Technological Context:**
* **Pre-Harappan:** In some areas, it preceded the Bronze Age Harappan culture.
* **Post-Harappan:** In many parts of India, it followed the mature Harappan phase.


* **Nature of Settlements:** Unlike the urban Harappans, Chalcolithic people were primarily **rural communities** settled in hilly lands and river valleys across South-Eastern Rajasthan, Western MP, Western Maharashtra, and Eastern India.

**Regional Cultures:**

* **South-Eastern Rajasthan (Banas Valley):**
* **Sites:** Ahar and Gilund (dry zones).
* **Ahar Culture (c. 2100–1500 BC):** Originally called *Tambavati* (place of copper). People practiced smelting and metallurgy from the start. Ahar is unique for having virtually no microlithic tools; stone axes/blades are absent.


* **Western MP (Malwa):**
* **Sites:** Kayatha, Eran, Navdatoli.
* **Malwa Culture (c. 1700–1200 BC):** Known for **Malwa-ware**, considered the richest Chalcolithic ceramics.


* **Western Maharashtra (Jorwe Culture):**
* **Timeline:** c. 1400–700 BC.
* **Sites:** Jorwe, Nevasa, Daimabad, Chandoli, Songaon, Inamgaon, Prakash, Nasik.
* **Geography:** Semi-arid areas with brown-black soil.
* **Urban Elements:** Sites like **Daimabad** and **Inamgaon** reached a proto-urban stage. Daimabad (20 hectares) was fortified with mud walls and bastions.


* **Eastern India:**
* **Sites:** Chirand (Bihar), Pandu Rajar Dhibi (West Bengal). Used more stone tools and fewer copper ones. Fish hooks found here suggest a diet of fish and rice.



---

#### **Economy and Livelihood**

**Agriculture:**

* **Crops:** Produced wheat, rice, and *bajra*.
* **Pulses:** Lentil (*masur*), black gram, green gram, grass pea.
* **Key Site:** **Navdatoli** (Narmada bank) has yielded the highest variety of food grains in India.
* **Textiles:** Produced cotton (Deccan black soil), *rai*, *bajra*, and millets. Spindle whorls in Malwa and silk threads in Maharashtra indicate expertise in spinning and weaving.

**Animal Husbandry:**

* **Domesticated Animals:** Cows, sheep, goats, pigs, buffaloes.
* **Diet:** Beef and pork were eaten.
* **Horse:** Generally not acquainted with the horse (remains are doubtful).
* **Dairy Limitation:** Likely did not milk animals for dairy, using them primarily for meat (similar to Gonds of Bastar).

---

#### **Architecture and Society**

**Housing:**

* **Materials:** Mostly mud-brick or wattle-and-daub with thatched roofs.
* **Exceptions:**
* **Ahar:** Lived in **stone houses**.
* **Gilund:** Rare use of **burnt bricks** (~1500 BC).


* **Inamgaon:** Large mud houses with ovens and circular pit houses. Later phase shows a massive 5-room house (likely a chief's residence) next to a granary.

**Social Inequality:**

* **Hierarchy:**
* **Settlement Size:** Large settlements (e.g., Daimabad, 20 ha) dominated smaller ones (5 ha).
* **Housing:** Chiefs lived in rectangular houses at the center; commoners in round huts.
* **Burials:** Rich graves contained copper necklaces and semi-precious beads; poor graves contained only pots.
* **Infant Mortality:** High rate of child burials suggests poor nutrition and lack of medical knowledge.



**Religion:**

* **Mother Goddess:** Terracotta figures found in Inamgaon (similar to West Asia).
* **Bull Cult:** Stylized bull terracottas in Malwa/Rajasthan.
* **Burial Practices:**
* **Maharashtra:** Dead buried in urns under house floors (North-South position).
* **South India:** East-West position.
* **Eastern India:** Fractional burial.



---

#### **The Ganeshwar Complex (Rajasthan)**

* **Timeline:** 2800–2200 BC (Pre-Harappan).
* **Location:** Near Khetri copper belt.
* **Significance:** Supplied copper objects to Harappa but did not urbanize itself. Primarily a hunting/agricultural society using microliths and copper tools.

---

#### **The Copper Hoards and OCP Culture**

**Period:** c. 2000–1500 BC.

* **Copper Hoards:** Over 80 hoards found (Rings, celts, harpoons, swords).
* **Concentration:** Ganga-Yamuna *doab*.
* **Largest Hoard:** **Gungeria (MP)** – 424 copper tools and 102 silver sheets.


* **Ochre-Coloured Pottery (OCP):** Found in association with copper hoards in Western UP.
* **Settlements:** Small, short-lived settlements (e.g., **Jodhpura**). Likely ended due to water-logging.



---

#### **Limitations of Chalcolithic Cultures**

Despite being food producers, these cultures had significant weaknesses:

1. **Rural Nature:** Lived in villages, not cities.
2. **Primitive Agriculture:** Lacked ploughs/hoes; used digging sticks with stone weights. Could not extensively cultivate hard black soil.
3. **Metallurgy:** **No Bronze.** They did not know how to mix tin with copper, unlike the Harappans. Copper tools were pliant.
4. **Illiteracy:** They did not know writing.
