# Central Asian Contact and Mutual Impact

## I. Political Aspects

Around **200 BC**, the large Mauryan empire was succeeded by native rulers (Shungas, Kanvas, Satavahanas) in the east/Deccan, and by a series of invaders from Central Asia in the north-west.

### 1. The Indo-Greeks (Bactrian Greeks)

Driven by pressure from Scythian tribes (who were pushed back by the **Great Wall of China**), the Greeks ruling Bactria (North Afghanistan) invaded India.

* **Territory:** They occupied a large part of north-western India, pushing as far as Ayodhya and Pataliputra, though they failed to establish united rule.
* **Menander (Milinda) (165-145 BC):** The most famous ruler.
* **Capital:** Sakala (modern Sialkot).
* **Religion:** Converted to Buddhism by **Nagasena** (Nagarjuna). Their dialogue is recorded in the text ***Milinda Panho*** (Questions of Milinda).


* **Significance:**
* **Coins:** First rulers to issue coins definitively attributed to specific kings. First to issue **gold coins** in India.
* **Art:** Introduced Hellenistic art features, leading to the **Gandhara** school.



### 2. The Shakas (Scythians)

The Shakas followed the Greeks, controlling a much larger territory with five distinct branches (Afghanistan, Taxila, Mathura, Western India, Upper Deccan).

* **Vikramaditya Legend:** In **57-58 BC**, a king of Ujjain defeated the Shakas and adopted the title *Vikramaditya*. This victory marked the beginning of the **Vikrama Samvat** era.
* **Rudradaman I (AD 130-150):** The most famous Shaka ruler (Western India branch).
* **Achievements:** Ruled Sindh, Gujarat, Malwa; repaired the **Sudarshana Lake** (Mauryan era irrigation work).
* **Language:** Despite being of Central Asian origin, he issued the first-ever long inscription in **chaste Sanskrit** (Junagadh inscription).



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **The Vikram Samvat:** The calendar era started by the victory over the Shakas (57 BC) is still widely used in India and is the official calendar system of Nepal.

### 3. The Parthians (Pahlavas)

Originally from Iran, they occupied a small portion of north-western India in the 1st Century AD.

* **Famous King:** **Gondophernes**.
* **Christianity:** St. Thomas is said to have arrived in India to propagate Christianity during his reign.

### 4. The Kushans (Yuechis/Tocharians)

Nomadic people from the steppes near China, they displaced the Shakas and created a massive empire extending from the **Oxus to the Ganges** (Central Asia to Bihar).

* **The Empire:** Spanned 9 modern countries. It created a unique opportunity for cultural interaction.
* **Dynasties:**
1. **House of Kadphises:** Kadphises I (copper coins) and Kadphises II (gold coins).
2. **House of Kanishka:** Extended power over upper India.


* **Kanishka:** The most famous ruler.
* **Shaka Era:** Started an era in **AD 78**.
* **Capitals:** **Purushapura** (Peshawar) and **Mathura**.
* **Religion:** Patronized Buddhism; held a Buddhist council in Kashmir legitimizing **Mahayana** Buddhism.



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **National Calendar:** The **Shaka Era** (starting AD 78), initiated by Kanishka, is the era used by the **Government of India** for its official national calendar today.

### 5. The Indo-Sassanians

By the mid-3rd Century AD, the Sassanians from Iran occupied the lower Indus.

* **"Hindustan":** A Sassanian inscription of **AD 262** uses the term *Hindustan* for the Indus region—the first recorded use of the term used in Mughal and modern times.

## II. Cultural Consequences

### 1. Structures and Pottery

* **Construction:** Use of **burnt bricks** for flooring and walls; use of tiles for roofing.
* **Pottery:** Characteristic Red Ware (plain and polished), sprinklers, and spouted channels (similar to Central Asian styles).

### 2. Military and Cavalry Technology

The Shakas and Kushans revolutionized warfare in India.

* **The Horse:** Introduced better cavalry and widespread use of riding horses.
* **Equipment:** Popularized **reins, saddles**, and the **toe stirrup** (made of rope).
* **Attire:** Introduced the **turban, tunic, trousers, heavy long coats**, caps, helmets, and boots.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Clothing:** The text notes that the *Sherwani* (worn in weddings today) is a successor of the heavy long coat introduced by these Central Asian invaders. The turban also remains a staple in Afghan and Punjabi culture.

### 3. Trade and Agriculture

* **The Silk Route:** Kushans controlled the Silk Route (China to Roman Empire via Central Asia/Iran). Tolls from this trade brought immense prosperity.
* **Gold:** Large influx of gold from the Altai mountains and trade with Rome. Kushans were the first to issue gold coins on a **wide scale**.
* **Agriculture:** Earliest archaeological traces of large-scale irrigation in Pakistan/Afghanistan date to the Kushan period.

### 4. Polity and Society

* **Feudatory System:** The Kushan title **"King of Kings"** indicates a hierarchy where they collected tribute from numerous small princes.
* **Divine Kingship:** Adopted the Chinese title "Son of Heaven" (Sons of God), strengthening the divine origin of kingship.
* **Satrap System:** Empire divided into Satrapies (provinces) ruled by Satraps.
* **Social Assimilation:** These foreign conquerors were completely Indianized. Manu classified them as "fallen Kshatriyas" (second-class warriors) to fit them into the Varna system.

### 5. Religious Developments

* **Vaishnavism:** Some foreigners adopted the worship of Vishnu. The Greek ambassador **Heliodorus** set up a pillar in honor of Vasudeva at Besnagar (MP).
* **Buddhism:**
* **Mahayana (The Great Vehicle):** Arose due to the influx of wealth and foreigners (who were non-vegetarians). Monks accepted gold, wore elaborate robes, and started **image worship** of the Buddha.
* **Hinayana:** The older, puritan school.
* **Kanishka’s Council:** Finalized Mahayana doctrines; commentaries were engraved on copper sheets and stored in a Stupa.



### 6. Art and Architecture

Two distinct schools flourished under foreign patronage:

* **Gandhara Art (North-West):** Synthesis of local and Indian elements with **Graeco-Roman style**. Famous for images of the Buddha with Greek facial features and hair.
* **Mathura School (Indigenous):** Produced distinct red sandstone images. Famous for the headless statue of Kanishka and the first stone images of **Vardhamana Mahavira**.

### 7. Language, Literature, and Science

* **Language:** Kushans used Greek, Kharoshthi, and Brahmi scripts. They began using **Sanskrit** for inscriptions (e.g., Rudradaman’s Junagadh inscription).
* **Literature:**
* **Ashvaghosha:** Wrote *Buddhacharita* (biography of Buddha) and *Saundarananda* (Sanskrit Kavya).
* **Kamasutra:** Written by Vatsyayana (3rd Century AD), earliest work on erotics and city life (*Nagaraka*).


* **Theatre:** Use of the curtain (*Yavanika*—derived from *Yavana*/Greek) suggests Greek influence.
* **Science & Astrology:**
* Greek influence on astrology (Term *Horoscope* -> Sanskrit *Horashastra*).
* **Medicine:** **Charaka** and **Sushruta** (focus on plants/*oshadhi*).
* **Technology:** Leather shoe making began; glass-making progressed significantly.
