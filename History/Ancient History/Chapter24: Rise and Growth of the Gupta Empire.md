# Rise and Growth of the Gupta Empire

## 1. Background

After the decline of the Kushans (North) and Satavahanas (Deccan) in the mid-3rd century, the **Gupta Empire** emerged, keeping North India politically united for over a century (AD 335–455).

* **Origin:** Likely **Vaishyas** (as the suffix *Gupta* implies "protected," a title recommended for Vaishyas in Dharmashastras).
* **Original Kingdom:** Comprised UP and Bihar. UP was the center of operations (Prayag/Allahabad) from where they expanded.
* **Relationship with Kushans:** Likely feudatories of the Kushans in UP. They adopted Kushan military techniques (saddle, reins, buttoned coats, boots) which gave them mobility.
* **Material Advantages:**
* Fertile land of **Madhyadesh** (Bihar/UP).
* Iron ores of Central India and South Bihar.
* Proximity to Silk Trade with the Byzantine (Eastern Roman) Empire.



---

## 2. Chandragupta I (AD 319–334)

The first important king.

* **Marriage Alliance:** Married a **Lichchhavi princess** from Nepal. This marriage into a Kshatriya family lent prestige to the Vaishya Guptas.
* **The Gupta Era:** Started the **Gupta Era in AD 319–20** to mark his accession.

---

## 3. Samudragupta (AD 335–380)

The son of Chandragupta I, known for violence and conquest (unlike the peace-loving Ashoka).

* **Source:** The **Allahabad Pillar Inscription** (*Prayag Prashasti*), composed by his court poet **Harishena**. It enumerates his conquests on the very same pillar that carries Ashoka’s edicts.
* **Five Groups of Conquests:**
1. **Ganga-Yamuna Doab:** Princes defeated and kingdoms incorporated.
2. **Frontier States:** Eastern Himalayas, Nepal, Assam, Bengal, and Punjab republics (crushed).
3. **Forest Kingdoms (*Atavika Rajyas*):** Vindhya region brought under control.
4. **South India:** 12 rulers of Eastern Deccan/South India conquered and liberated (suzerainty acknowledged as far as Kanchi).
5. **Shakas/Kushans:** Distant rulers (some in Afghanistan) submitted.


* **International Relations:** **Meghavarman** (ruler of Sri Lanka) sent a missionary to build a Buddhist temple at Gaya.
* **Title:** Called the **"Napoleon of India"** for his bravery and generalship.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **The Napoleon Comparison:** The title "Napoleon of India" was famously coined by the historian **V.A. Smith**. It remains a popular epithet in Indian history textbooks today, highlighting Samudragupta's undefeated military record.

---

## 4. Chandragupta II (AD 380–412)

The reign marked the high watermark of the empire.

* **Marriage Alliance:** Married his daughter **Prabhavati** to a **Vakataka** prince (Brahmana caste) in Central India. After the prince's death, Prabhavati ruled as regent, giving Chandragupta indirect control over Central India.
* **Conquests:**
* Used Vakataka influence to conquer **Mathura** and **Western Malwa/Gujarat** from the Shaka Kshatrapas (ending 400 years of Shaka rule).
* This gave access to the western sea coast and trade wealth.


* **Capitals:** Pataliputra and **Ujjain** (Second Capital).
* **Title:** Adopted the title **Vikramaditya** (Sun of Valour). He proved to be a greater *Shakari* (Enemy of Shakas) than the original ruler of 57 BC.
* **Court:** Adorned by scholars like **Kalidasa** and **Amarasimha**.
* **Iron Pillar:** The inscription on the **Mehrauli Iron Pillar** (Delhi) glorifies a king named "Chandra," believed to be him.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **The Rust-Free Pillar:** The Iron Pillar at Mehrauli (Delhi), attributed to Chandragupta II, is a metallurgical marvel. Despite standing in the open for 1,600 years, it has not rusted, baffling modern scientists and standing as a testament to ancient Indian metallurgy.

* **Foreign Account:** Chinese pilgrim **Fa-hsien** (AD 399–414) visited India during his reign.

---

## 5. Fall of the Empire

The decline began in the second half of the 5th century.

### **Causes of Decline**

1. **Huna Invasions:**
* **Skandagupta** initially checked them, but successors were weak.
* Hunas (excellent archers/horsemen with metal stirrups) occupied Eastern Malwa and Central India by AD 485.


2. **Rise of Feudatories:**
* **Yashodharman of Malwa:** Defeated Hunas but also challenged Gupta authority, setting up a victory pillar in AD 532 claiming conquest of North India.
* **Maukharis** (Bihar/UP) and **Maitrakas** (Valabhi/Gujarat) became independent.


3. **Economic Decline:**
* Loss of Western India deprived Guptas of trade revenues.
* **Guild Migration:** Silk weavers migrated from Gujarat to Malwa (AD 473) and abandoned their craft, indicating a drop in demand for luxury cloth.
* **Debasement of Coinage:** Later Gupta kings reduced the gold content in coins to maintain currency, but failed.
* Land grants reduced state revenue, making it hard to maintain a professional army.
