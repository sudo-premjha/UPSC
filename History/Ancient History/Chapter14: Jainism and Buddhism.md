### **Chapter 14: Jainism and Buddhism**

In the **6th–5th centuries BC**, numerous religious sects (~62) arose in the mid-Gangetic plains. Of these, **Jainism** and **Buddhism** emerged as the most potent reform movements.

#### **Causes of Origin**

1. **Varna System Tensions:**
* Society was divided into four varnas. **Brahmanas** claimed supreme privileges (tax exemption). **Kshatriyas** (rulers) reacted against this domination. Both Mahavira and Buddha were Kshatriyas who disputed Brahmana authority.
* **Vaishyas** (traders/agriculturists) were the principal taxpayers but ranked third. They sought better status.
* **Shudras** were marginalized and served the upper three varnas.


2. **Agricultural Economy:**
* **Iron Tools:** The spread of iron axes allowed clearing of dense forests in Eastern UP/Bihar (~600 BC).
* **Cattle Protection:** The new agrarian economy required bullocks for ploughing. However, Vedic sacrifices involved indiscriminate cattle killing. New religions preached non-violence (*Ahimsa*) to protect cattle wealth.


3. **Urbanization and Trade:**
* **New Cities:** Rise of cities like Rajgir, Pataliputra, Vaishali, Varanasi, Kaushambi around **500 BC**.
* **Coins:** Earliest punch-marked coins (5th C. BC) facilitated trade.
* **Merchant Support:** Vaishyas (*Setthis*) supported Buddhism/Jainism because these religions preached non-violence (good for trade) and did not condemn usury (unlike Dharmasutras).


4. **Reaction Against Private Property:**
* The accumulation of new wealth (coins, property) created inequality and misery. Common people yearned for a return to a simpler, ascetic lifestyle, which the new religions advocated.



---

#### **Vardhamana Mahavira and Jainism**

* **Tirthankaras:** Mahavira was the **24th Tirthankara**. His predecessor, **Parshvanatha** (23rd), was a historical figure from Banaras.
* **Mahavira's Life:**
* Born: **540 BC** (near Vaishali). Father was a Kshatriya chief; mother was a Lichchhavi princess (sister of Chetaka).
* Quest: Left home at 30. Attained *Kaivalya* (omniscience) at 42.
* Death: **468 BC** at **Pavapuri** (near Rajgir) at age 72.


* **Doctrines:**
* **Five Vows:** 1. Non-violence (*Ahimsa*). 2. Truth. 3. Non-stealing. 4. Non-hoarding. 5. **Brahmacharya** (Added by Mahavira).
* **Triratna (Three Jewels):** Right Knowledge, Right Faith, Right Action.
* **Extremism:** Mahavira asked followers to discard all clothes (unlike Parshva who allowed covering). This led to the split into **Digambaras** (naked) and **Shvetambaras** (white-clad).


* **Spread:** Spread to South India (Karnataka) under **Bhadrabahu** (Chandragupta Maurya tradition) and Kalinga (under **Kharavela**). It survived in Western India (Gujarat/Rajasthan) among trader communities.

---

#### **Gautama Buddha and Buddhism**

* **Buddha's Life:**
* Born: **567 BC** (Lumbini, Nepal). Father was a Shakya ruler.
* Quest: Left home at 29. Attained Enlightenment at 35 (**Bodh-Gaya**).
* Preaching: First sermon at **Sarnath**. Wandered for 40 years.
* Death: **487 BC** at **Kusinagara** (UP) at age 80.


* **Doctrines:**
* **Four Noble Truths:** World is full of sorrow caused by desire. Conquering desire leads to *Nirvana*.
* **Eightfold Path (*Ashtangika Marga*):** Right observation, determination, speech, action, livelihood, effort, awareness, concern.
* **Middle Path:** Avoid excess luxury and excess austerity.
* **Code of Conduct:** Non-violence, no stealing, no intoxicants, truthfulness.



---

#### **Spread and Decline of Buddhism**

* **Reasons for Spread:**
* **Liberal:** Attacked Varna system; admitted women and lower castes.
* **Language:** Preached in **Pali** (language of the people).
* **Royal Patronage:** Adopted by **Ashoka** (spread to Central/West Asia, Sri Lanka), Kanishka, and Harsha.


* **Reasons for Decline:**
* **Brahmanical Reform:** Brahmanism adopted cattle protection and opened access to women/Shudras.
* **Corruption:** Monasteries became wealthy and corrupt. Monks gave up Pali for Sanskrit.
* **Vajrayana:** Tantric practices and loose morals degenerated the faith.
* **Persecution:** By Pushyamitra Shunga, Huna king **Mihirakula**, and Shaivite **Shashanka** (felled Bodhi tree).
* **Turkish Invasions (12th C):** Monasteries (e.g., Nalanda) were looted and monks killed; survivors fled to Nepal/Tibet.



---

#### **Significance and Legacy**

* **Social Impact:**
* Challenged Varna/Caste. Empowered women and Shudras.
* Boosted cattle wealth through non-violence (later adopted by Hinduism).


* **Cultural Impact:**
* **Literature:** Enriched Pali and Apabhramsha.
* **Education:** Residential universities like **Nalanda**, **Vikramashila**, **Valabhi**.
* **Art:** First human statues worshipped (Buddha). Development of **Gandhara** (Greek-Indian mix) and Mathura art schools. Cave architecture (Barabar, Nasik, Ajanta).
