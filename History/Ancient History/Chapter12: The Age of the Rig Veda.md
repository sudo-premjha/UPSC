### **Chapter 12: The Age of the Rig Veda**

#### **Arrival of the Indo-Aryans**

The Indo-Iranians (ancestors of Indo-Aryans and Iranians) moved towards India from two major cultural zones in Central Asia:

1. **Andronovo Culture (2nd Millennium BC):** Covered almost the whole of Central Asia. Features: Stock breeding, horse use, spoked wheels, cremation, pit-dwellings (birch-roofed), Soma drink. Regarded as **Proto-Indo-Iranian**.
2. **Bactria-Margiana Archaeological Complex (BMAC) (1900–1500 BC):** Covered South Central Asia (Bactria/Balkh in Afghanistan, Margiana in Turkmenistan/Uzbekistan). Around 1500 BC, it shows evidence of horses, chariots, and the Swastika motif.

**Migration:**

* From BMAC, herdsmen moved to the border of the Indian subcontinent.
* **Swat Valley:** Horse remains and post-cremation burials appear here from about **1400 BC**, linking to Central Asian movements.
* **Geography:** The earliest Aryans settled in Eastern Afghanistan, North-West Frontier Province, Punjab, and fringes of Western UP.
* **Rivers:** The *Sindhu* (Indus) is the river par excellence. The *Sarasvati* is the best of rivers (*Naditama*), identified with the **Ghaggar-Hakra** channel (though the name originally likely referred to the Helmand river in Afghanistan).

**Inscriptional Evidence (Pre-India):**

* **Iraq:** Inscription from **2200 BC**. Kassite inscriptions (~1600 BC).
* **Anatolia:** Hittite inscriptions (19th–17th C. BC).
* **Syria:** Mitanni inscriptions (14th C. BC).
* **Greece:** Mycenaean inscriptions (~1400 BC).
* *Note:* No such early Aryan inscriptions have been found within India.

---

#### **The Rig Veda**

* **Nature:** The earliest text of the Indo-European languages (c. 1500 BC). Contains prayers to gods like Agni, Indra, Mitra, Varuna.
* **Composition:** 10 Mandalas (Books II-VII are oldest; I and X are latest).
* **Language:** Sanskrit, but includes **Munda and Dravidian words** (likely adopted from Harappans). Similarities with the Iranian *Avesta*.
* **Identity:** The term *Arya* occurs 36 times, denoting a cultural/linguistic community.

---

#### **Conflict and Warfare**

**Adversaries:**

* **Dasas:** Mentioned in ancient Iranian literature; likely early Aryan kin. Treated softly.
* **Dasyus:** Original inhabitants (phallus worshippers, non-dairy cattle herders). Treated with hostility (*Dasyuhatya* = slaughter of Dasyus).
* **Technology:** Aryans won due to **horses, chariots** (spoked wheels), and coats of mail (*Varman*). **Indra** is called *Purandara* (destroyer of dwelling units).

**Intra-Tribal Conflict:**

* **Battle of Ten Kings:** Fought on the river **Parushni** (Ravi).
* **Victor:** Sudas (Bharata clan, supported by priest Vasishtha).
* **Losers:** 10 Chiefs (5 Aryan + 5 Non-Aryan).
* **Result:** Bharatas joined with the defeated **Purus** to form the **Kurus**, dominating the Upper Gangetic basin.



---

#### **Material Life**

**Economy:** Predominantly **pastoral**.

* **Cattle:** The most important wealth. Wars were called *Gavishthi* (search for cows). Priests received cows/women slaves, not land.
* **Agriculture:** Known but secondary. Ploughshare mentioned (likely wood).
* **Crafts:** Carpenters, chariot-makers, weavers, leather workers, potters.
* **Metals:** *Ayas* (Copper/Bronze) mentioned. No iron yet.
* **Settlements:** Not urban. *Pur* meant a dwelling unit or fortified mud settlement, not a city. Lived in mud houses (e.g., 13-room house at **Bhagwanpura**, 1600–1000 BC).

---

#### **Tribal Polity**

* **King (*Rajan*):** A tribal chief, not an absolute monarch.
* **Role:** Protector of cattle, leader in war.
* **Election:** Traces of election by the tribal assembly (*Samiti*) exist.
* **Revenue:** Voluntary offerings (*Bali*), not regular taxes.


* **Assemblies:** **Sabha, Samiti, Vidatha, Gana**. Women attended Sabha and Vidatha.
* **Officials:** *Purohita* (Priest - Vasishtha/Vishvamitra), *Senani* (Army head), *Vrajapati* (Pasture officer). No standing army; tribal militia used.

---

#### **Social Structure**

* **Tribal Loyalty:** To the *Jana* (Tribe) and *Vis* (Clan). The term *Janapada* (Territory) is absent.
* **Family:** Patriarchal joint family (*Griha*). Desire for sons (warriors) was paramount.
* **Women:** Could attend assemblies and compose hymns. Widow remarriage and levirate practiced. No child marriage (marriage age 16-17).
* **Varna System:**
* Initially based on color (*Varna*): Fair Aryans vs. Dark Indigenous.
* Three classes: Warriors, Priests, Commoners.
* **Shudras:** Appear only in the late **10th Mandala** of the Rig Veda.
* **Slavery:** Domestic slavery existed (mostly women), not agricultural slavery.



---

#### **Religion**

* **Nature Worship:** Personified natural forces.
* **Major Gods:**
* **Indra (250 Hymns):** Warlord/Rain God.
* **Agni (200 Hymns):** Fire God, intermediary between man and gods.
* **Varuna:** Upholder of cosmic order (*Rita*).
* **Soma:** God of plants (intoxicating drink).


* **Rituals:** Prayers (collective/individual) and simple sacrifices (milk, grain) for material gains (cows, sons), not spiritual upliftment.
