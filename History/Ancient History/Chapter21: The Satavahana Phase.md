# The Satavahana Phase

## 1. Political History

In the Deccan and Central India, the **Satavahanas** succeeded the Mauryas (after a gap of 100 years).

* **Identity:** They are identified with the **Andhras** mentioned in the Puranas.
* **Origin:** While Puranas call them Andhras, their earliest inscriptions and coins are found in **North Maharashtra** (upper Godavari valley), not Andhra.
* **Rivals:** The **Shakas** (Scythians) were their greatest competitors in Western India.



### **Key Rulers**

1. **Gautamiputra Satakarni (AD 106-130):**
* Restored the dynasty's fortunes.
* Called himself the **"Only Brahmana"** (*Eka Bamhana*).
* Defeated the Shaka ruler **Nahapana** (proven by over 8,000 Shaka coins found in Nasik re-struck by Gautamiputra).
* Empire: Malwa (North) to Karnataka (South).


2. **Vashishthiputra Pulumayi (AD 130-154):**
* Set up the capital at **Paithan** (Pratishthan) on the Godavari.
* Expanded into Andhra.


3. **Yajna Sri Satakarni (AD 165-194):**
* Last great king.
* **Navigator King:** Enthusiastic about trade and navigation; his coins feature a **ship**. Recovered Konkan and Malwa from Shakas.



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Paithan:** The ancient capital *Pratishthan* is today's **Paithan** in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra. It remains famous to this day for its traditional **Paithani sarees**, a legacy of the rich weaving craft mentioned in ancient texts.

---

## 2. Material Culture and Economy

The material culture was a fusion of local (Megalithic) elements and Northern ingredients.

### **Agriculture and Minerals**

* **Rice Bowl:** The Krishna-Godavari delta became a great rice bowl due to **paddy transplantation**.
* **Cotton:** Andhra was famous for cotton products.
* **Iron:** Exploited ores from Karimnagar and Warangal. Tools included socketed hoes, sickles, and ploughshares.
* **Gold:** Exploited the **Kolar fields** (Karnataka). Used as bullion (no gold coins issued).

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Kolar Gold Fields (KGF):** The text notes ancient gold workings in Kolar. This region remained the primary source of gold in India until the closure of the famous **Kolar Gold Fields** in the 21st century.

### **Currency and Urbanism**

* **Coins:** Mostly **Lead** (found in Deccan). Also issued Potin, Copper, and Bronze.
* **Urbanization:**
* Use of **Burnt Bricks** and **Flat Perforated Roof Tiles** (seen at Peddabankur).
* 22 brick wells and covered underground drains found at Peddabankur (Karimnagar).
* Pliny mentioned 30 walled towns in the Andhra country.

## 3. Social Organization

### **Structure**

* **Tribal to Brahmanical:** Satavahanas were originally a Deccan tribe but became Brahmanized.
* **Caste System:** Gautamiputra claimed to stop the intermixture of social orders (*Varnas*), likely referring to the assimilation of Shakas (foreigners) and local tribes.
* **Matrilineal Traces:**
* Kings were named after their mothers (e.g., **Gautamiputra** = Son of Gautami; **Vashishthiputra** = Son of Vashishthi).
* Queens acted as regents and made religious gifts.
* *Note:* Basic succession remained patriarchal (throne passed to the son).



### **Rise of Merchants**

* Merchants and artisans prospered and made donations.
* **Gandhikas:** Perfumers (*Gandhikas*) are repeatedly mentioned as donors. Later, the term became general for shopkeepers.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **The Name "Gandhi":** The text explicitly states that the modern title/surname **Gandhi** is derived from the ancient term **Gandhika** (perfumer), showing how an occupational title evolved into a common surname.

---

## 4. Pattern of Administration

The Satavahanas retained Mauryan structures but introduced **military and feudal** traits.

* **Divine Kingship:** Kings compared to gods/heroes (Rama, Arjuna, Bhima).
* **Officials:**
* **Senapati:** Appointed as Provincial Governor (indicating military rule).
* **Gaulmika:** Head of a rural military regiment (9 chariots, 9 elephants, 25 horses, 45 foot-soldiers). Administered the countryside.


* **Land Grants:**
* **First Rulers** to make land grants to Brahmanas and Buddhist monks.
* Granted lands were tax-free and free from entry by royal police/soldiers, creating small autonomous islands (beginnings of feudalism).


* **Feudatories:** Graded hierarchy:
1. **Raja** (Minted coins).
2. **Mahabhoja**.
3. **Senapati**.



---

## 5. Religion

The rulers were Brahmanas, but they patronized Buddhism.

* **Brahmanism:** Kings performed *Ashvamedha* and *Vajapeya* sacrifices and worshipped Krishna/Vasudeva.
* **Buddhism:**
* Flourished in **Nasik/Junar** (supported by traders) and **Nagarjunakonda/Amaravati** (supported by artisans).
* Mahayana Buddhism commanded a large following.



---

## 6. Architecture

Famous for rock-cut architecture and Stupas.

### **Rock-Cut Architecture (Western Deccan)**

* **Chaitya:** Buddhist temple/prayer hall.
* **Karle Chaitya:** Most impressive; 40m long, cut from solid rock.


* **Vihara:** Monastery (residence for monks).
* Nasik has three Viharas with inscriptions of Nahapana and Gautamiputra.



### **Stupas (Andhra Region)**

* **Amaravati Stupa:** Began c. 200 BC, fully reconstructed in 2nd Century AD. Full of sculptures depicting the Buddha's life.
* **Nagarjunakonda:** Richest area for structures; contained Stupas, Mahachaityas, and early Brahmanical brick temples.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Amaravati:** The ancient center of Buddhist art, Amaravati, lends its name to the **new capital city** of the modern Indian state of Andhra Pradesh.

---

## 7. Language and Literature

* **Official Language:** **Prakrit**.
* **Script:** **Brahmi**.
* **Literature:**
* **Gathasattasai (Gathasaptasati):** A collection of 700 erotic/romantic verses in Prakrit, attributed to the Satavahana King **Hala**.

## 8. Chronology

| Time Period | Event |
| --- | --- |
| **1st Millennium BC** | Iron working in Karimnagar/Warangal (Megalithic phase). |
| **Pre-Christian Era** | Gold workings in Kolar fields. |
| **c. 200 BC** | Construction begins at Amaravati Stupa and Chaityas in NW Deccan. |
| **1st Century BC** | Earliest Satavahana inscriptions; defeat of Kanvas. |
| **AD 106-130** | Reign of **Gautamiputra Satakarni** (Defeated Shakas). |
| **AD 130-150** | Reign of Shaka ruler **Rudradaman I** (Defeated Satavahanas). |
| **AD 130-154** | Reign of **Vashishthiputra Pulumayi** (Capital at Paithan). |
| **AD 165-194** | Reign of **Yajna Sri Satakarni** (Ship coins). |
| **AD 220** | End of Satavahana Kingdom. |
| **3rd Century AD** | Rise of **Ikshvakus** in Eastern Deccan (Nagarjunakonda). |
