# Developments in Philosophy

## 1. Goals of Life

Ancient thinkers advocated four major goals for human existence:

1. **Dharma:** Regulation of the social order (Subject of *Dharmashastras*).
2. **Artha:** Economic resources (Subject of *Arthashastra*).
3. **Kama:** Physical pleasures (Subject of *Kamasutra*).
4. **Moksha:** Salvation/Deliverance from the cycle of birth and death.

While the first three dealt with the material world, **Moksha** became the central subject of *Darshana* (Philosophy).

---

## 2. The Six Schools of Philosophy (*Shad-Darshana*)

By the beginning of the Christian era, six major schools of philosophy developed.

### **A. Samkhya (Count/Enumeration)**

* **Origin:** The earliest school. Kapila is the earliest exponent.
* **Philosophy:**
* **Materialistic Roots:** Initially believed the world owes its creation to **Nature (*Prakriti*)** rather than God (a rational/scientific view).
* **Evolution:** Around the 4th Century AD, **Spirit (*Purusha*)** was added. Creation was then attributed to the interaction of both Prakriti and Purusha.


* **Method:** Knowledge is acquired through Perception (*Pratyaksha*), Inference (*Anumana*), and Hearing (*Shabda*).

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Ayurveda:** The theoretical foundation of Ayurveda (the Indian system of medicine) is deeply rooted in **Samkhya** philosophy, particularly the concepts of *Prakriti* (constitution) and the evolution of elements.

### **B. Yoga (Application)**

* **Path to Salvation:** Through meditation and physical application.
* **Practice:** Control over senses and bodily organs.
* **Asanas:** Physical postures.
* **Pranayama:** Breathing exercises.


* **Significance:** Presupposed knowledge of physiology and anatomy. It offered a way to divert the mind from worldly matters to achieve concentration.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Global Phenomenon:** Yoga is India's most significant cultural export today. While the ancient text focused on salvation (*Moksha*), modern Yoga is widely practiced for physical health and stress relief, recognized globally (e.g., International Day of Yoga).

### **C. Nyaya (Analysis/Logic)**

* **Focus:** System of **Logic**.
* **Method:** Salvation through knowledge. The veracity of a statement is tested through inference, hearing, and analogy.
* **Syllogism Example:**
1. There is fire in the mountain.
2. Because it emits smoke.
3. Whatever emits smoke contains fire.



### **D. Vaisheshika (Particularities)**

* **Focus:** Material elements (*Dravya*).
* **Atomic Theory:** Propounded that all material objects are made of **atoms** (making it the beginning of physics in India).
* **Elements:** Earth, water, fire, air, and ether combine to form new objects.
* **Limitation:** The scientific view was diluted by a belief in God and heaven.

### **E. Mimamsa (Interpretation)**

* **Focus:** Justification of **Vedic rituals**.
* **Philosophy:** The Vedas contain eternal truth. Salvation depends on the performance of Vedic sacrifices.
* **Social Impact:** It legitimized the social distance between Varnas and maintained the ritual authority of Brahmanas.

### **F. Vedanta (End of Veda)**

* **Basis:** *Brahmasutra* of Badarayana (2nd Century BC) and the Upanishads.
* **Core Philosophy:**
* **Brahma** is the only reality; everything else is unreal (*Maya*).
* **Atma** (Self) coincides with **Brahma**.
* **Karma:** Actions in the present birth determine consequences in the next (linked to *Punarjanma*/Rebirth).


* **Commentaries:**
* **Shankara (9th C):** Considered Brahma without attributes (*Nirguna*); Knowledge (*Jnana*) is the path.
* **Ramanuja (12th C):** Considered Brahma with attributes (*Saguna*); Devotion (*Bhakti*) is the path.



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Hindu Identity:** Vedanta, particularly Advaita Vedanta (Shankara's non-dualism), forms the philosophical backbone of modern Hinduism as propagated by figures like Swami Vivekananda in the 19th and 20th centuries.

## 3. Charvaka and Materialism

While the six systems became idealistic, a distinct materialistic philosophy also existed.

* **Lokayata:** Philosophy derived from the common people (*Loka*).
* **Charvaka:** Main expounder.
* **Beliefs:** Opposed the quest for spiritual salvation. Denied God and Brahma. Accepted only what could be experienced by human senses.
* **Critique of Rituals:** Claimed Brahmanas manufactured rituals just to acquire gifts (*Dakshina*).
* **Famous Teaching:** "Eat, drink, and be merry" (borrow ghee to eat well if needed)—though this is often quoted by opponents to discredit him.


* **Decline:** Developed during the expanding economy (500 BC–AD 300) but was overshadowed by idealistic philosophies by the 5th Century AD, which hindered scientific inquiry.

## 4. Chronology

| Year | Event |
| --- | --- |
| **500 BC – AD 300** | Development of materialist philosophy (Charvaka/Lokayata). |
| **2nd C BC** | Compilation of the *Brahmasutra* of Badarayana (Basis of Vedanta). |
| **4th C AD** | Samkhya system modified: *Purusha* (Spirit) added to *Prakriti*. |
| **5th C AD** | Materialistic philosophy overshadowed by Idealistic philosophy. |
| **9th C AD** | **Shankara** writes commentary on *Brahmasutra*. |
| **12th C AD** | **Ramanuja** writes commentary on *Brahmasutra*. |
