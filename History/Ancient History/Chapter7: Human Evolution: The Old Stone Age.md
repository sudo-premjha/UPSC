### **Chapter 7: Human Evolution: The Old Stone Age**

#### **African Ancestors of Human Beings**

The earth is over **4600 million years old**. The evolution of its crust spans four stages, the last being the **Quaternary**, divided into two epochs:

* **Pleistocene (Ice Age):** 2 million BC – 12,000 BC.
* **Holocene (Post-Ice Age):** 12,000 BC – Present.

Humans appeared in the pre-Pleistocene and early Pleistocene times. Evolution unfolded through several key species:

| Species | Time Period | Brain Case (cc) | Key Characteristics |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Australopithecus** (Southern Ape) | 5.5 – 1.5 million years ago | 400 cc | **Proto-human.** Bipedal (walked on two legs), pot-bellied, mixture of ape and human traits. |
| **Homo Habilis** (Handy Man) | 2 – 1.5 million years ago | 500–700 cc | **First real human.** Lightly built braincase. Broke and sharpened stones to use as tools. |
| **Homo Erectus** (Upright Man) | 1.8 – 1.6 million years ago | 800–1200 cc | Strongly built skull. **Discovered fire.** Distinctive tool: **Hand Axe**. Traveled long distances (found in Africa, Asia, Europe). |
| **Homo Sapiens** (Wise Man) | 230,000 – 30,000 years ago | 1200–1800 cc | Resembles **Neanderthal Man**. Short body, narrow forehead. Evolved in Europe/Old World. |
| **Homo Sapiens Sapiens** (Modern Man) | 115,000 years ago (in Africa) | 1200–2000 cc | Large rounded braincase, large forehead, thinner bones. Emerged in Late Stone Age (Upper Palaeolithic). |

---

#### **The Early Man in India**

Fossils relating to human evolution in the subcontinent are rare.

* **Siwalik Hills (India/Pakistan):** Skulls of *Ramapithecus* (female) and *Sivapithecus* found in the Potwar plateau (Punjab, Pakistan) are dated to c. **2.2 million years old**. They possess hominid features but are largely considered apes that became extinct in the region.
* **Narmada Valley (India):** A nearly complete skull found at **Hathnora (MP)** in 1982. Originally called *Homo erectus*, it is now recognized as **archaic *Homo sapiens***.
* **Sri Lanka (Modern Humans):** Fossils from **Fa Hien cave** dated to **34,000 years ago** represent the earliest evidence of modern humans (*Homo sapiens sapiens*) in the subcontinent. This supports the theory of an early **coastal migration** from Africa (South to North) around 50,000 years ago.

---

#### **Phases in the Palaeolithic Age**

The Palaeolithic Age in India is divided into three phases based on tool types and climate.

| Phase | Time Period | Tools | Key Sites |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Lower Palaeolithic** (Early Old Stone Age) | 600,000 – 150,000 BC | **Hand axes**, cleavers, choppers (used for digging/skinning). | **Bori (Maharashtra)** (Earliest site), Belan Valley (UP), Bhimbetka (MP), Didwana (Rajasthan), Sohan Valley (Pakistan). |
| **Middle Palaeolithic** | 150,000 – 35,000 BC | Flake tools: Blades, points, borers, scrapers. | Narmada Valley, Tungabhadra River, Belan Valley (UP). |
| **Upper Palaeolithic** | 35,000 – 10,000 BC | **Blades and burins**, flint industries. | 566 sites found (Andhra, Karnataka, Maharashtra). **Bhimbetka** caves. |

* **Climate Context:** Lower Palaeolithic covers the greater part of the Ice Age. Upper Palaeolithic coincides with the last phase of the Ice Age when the climate became warmer and less humid.

---

#### **The Mesolithic Age: Hunters and Herders**

Starting around **9000 BC**, this intermediate stage bridges the Palaeolithic and Neolithic ages. It coincides with the **Holocene** epoch (warm and rainy climate).

* **Tools:** **Microliths** (tiny stone tools).
* **Subsistence:** Shift from hunting/gathering to **animal husbandry**.
* **Key Sites:**
* **Bagor (Rajasthan):** Distinctive microlithic industry. Occupied for 5,000 years.
* **Adamgarh (MP):** Earliest evidence of **animal domestication** (~5000 BC).
* **Sambhar (Rajasthan):** Evidence of **plant cultivation** (~7000–6000 BC).



---

#### **Art and Social Organization**

* **Prehistoric Art:**
* **Bhimbetka (MP):** Over 500 painted rock shelters spanning Upper Palaeolithic to Mesolithic.
* **Themes:** Hunting scenes involving birds, animals, and humans. Perching birds (grain-eaters) are absent in early paintings, confirming a non-agricultural economy.
* **Purpose:** Likely ritualistic to ensure control over wild animals for hunting.


* **Social Organization:**
* **Band Society:** Humans lived in small hunting bands (max ~25 people).
* **Alliances:** Bands likely formed alliances (max ~500 people) for mutual aid.
* **Sharing:** Resources were shared; no sharp social differentiation existed.
* **Language:** Originated around **50,000 BC**, facilitating communication and band formation.
