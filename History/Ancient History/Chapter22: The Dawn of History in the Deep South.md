# The Dawn of History in the Deep South

## 1. The Megalithic Background

Before the historical period (marked by writing, metal coins, and state formation) emerged in the tip of the peninsula (c. 2nd Century BC), the upland areas were inhabited by **Megalithic Builders**.

### **Characteristics of Megalithic Culture**

* **Time Period:** c. 1000 BC to 1st Century BC (persisted in some areas into the Christian era).
* **Megaliths:** Funerary structures (graves) encircled by large pieces of stone.
* **Contents:** Skeletons, pottery (Black-and-Red Ware), and iron objects.


* **Pottery:** **Black-and-Red Ware** was so common it was initially called "Megalithic Ware."
* **Livelihood:**
* **Agriculture:** Hoes and sickles found, but ploughshares are rare. Agriculture was limited; they did not settle on plains due to thick forests.
* **War/Hunting:** Arrowheads, spearheads, and tridents (associated with Shiva) outnumber agricultural tools.


* **Burial Practices:**
* **Urn Burial:** Common in Southern Tamil Nadu (red pottery urns in pits).
* **Stone Circles:** Common in Krishna-Godavari valley.



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **The Trident (Trishul):** The text mentions tridents found in these ancient graves. This symbol remains central to Shaivism in South India today, often seen in temples of Murugan and Shiva across Tamil Nadu and Kerala.

## 2. State Formation and Civilization

By the **2nd Century BC**, people moved from uplands to fertile river basins (deltaic areas) due to contact with the North (traders, conquerors, Jain/Buddhist missionaries).

* **Economic Drivers:**
* **Iron Technology:** Allowed forest clearance and plough agriculture.
* **Trade:** The route to the south (*Dakshinapatha*) was valued for gold, pearls, and precious stones.
* **Roman Trade:** Flourishing trade with the Roman Empire (especially Egypt) contributed to the formation of the three states: Cholas, Cheras, and Pandyas.


* **Northern Influence:**
* Ashokan inscriptions mention Cholas, Pandyas, Keralaputras, and Satyaputras.
* Heterodox sects (Buddhists/Jains/Ajivikas) were the early carriers of Gangetic culture to the south.



---

## 3. Three Early Kingdoms

The region south of the Krishna river was divided into three kingdoms.

### **A. The Pandyas**

* **Location:** Southern Tamil Nadu (Madurai, Tirunelveli).
* **Capital:** **Madurai**.
* **Key Features:**
* Famous for **pearls**.
* Mentioned by Megasthenes (ruled by a woman, indicating matriarchal influence).
* Profited from Roman trade; sent ambassadors to Emperor Augustus.



### **B. The Cholas**

* **Location:** North-east of Pandyas (Coromandel Coast), between Pennar and Velar rivers.
* **Capital:** **Uraiyur** (famous for cotton) and later **Puhar** (Kaveripattanam).
* **Key Rulers:**
* **Elara (2nd C. BC):** Conquered Sri Lanka.
* **Karikala (2nd C. AD):** Founded Puhar; built a 160 km embankment along the Kaveri using 12,000 Sri Lankan slaves.


* **Decline:** Collapsed under attacks from Pallavas; played a marginal role until the 9th Century.

### **C. The Cheras (Kerala)**

* **Location:** Narrow strip between sea and mountains (Kerala/Tamil Nadu).
* **Key Port:** **Muziris** (Cranganore). Romans had two regiments and a temple of Augustus here.
* **Key Ruler:** **Senguttuvan** (The Red/Good Chera).
* **Relations:** Constant wars with Cholas and Pandyas.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Muziris Heritage Project:** The ancient port of Muziris mentioned here is currently the site of a major archaeological and heritage preservation project by the Government of Kerala to showcase this ancient spice trade route.

## 4. The Economy: Purse and Sword

### **Sources of Revenue**

1. **Agriculture:** The foundation of the state. The Kaveri delta was so fertile that "the space in which an elephant could lie down produced enough to feed seven persons."
2. **Trade:** Customs duties collected at ports (like Puhar) and transit duties on roads.
3. **Spoils of War:** War booty was significant.

### **Exports to the West**

* Spices (Pepper), Ivory, Pearls, Precious Stones, Muslin, Silk.
* **Greek Etymology:** Greek words for rice (*Oryza*), ginger (*Zingiber*), and cinnamon are derived from Tamil.

### **Military**

* Army included chariots (ox-drawn), elephants, cavalry, and infantry.
* Horses were imported by sea into the Pandyan kingdom.

## 5. Society and Classes

### **Social Structure**

* **Ruling Class (*Arasar*):** Had marriage relations with rich peasants.
* **Peasants (*Vellalas*):** Held the bulk of land. Divided into rich and poor. Rich Vellalas held civil/military offices.
* **Labourers (*Kadaisiyar*):** Lowest class; status similar to slaves.
* **Brahmanas:** Appeared in the Sangam age. Functioned as poets and were generously rewarded with gold, land, and chariots. Tamil Brahmanas partook of meat and wine.
* **Inequality:** Sharp distinction between rich (brick houses) and poor (huts).

### **Religion**

* **Indigenous:** Chief god was **Murugan** (later Subramaniya).
* **Brahmanical:** Kings performed Vedic sacrifices. Worship of Vishnu is mentioned later.
* **Funerary:** Megalithic practices (offerings to dead) continued alongside cremation.

> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Murugan Worship:** Murugan remains the "Tamil God" par excellence. His six abodes (*Arupadaiveedu*) are major pilgrimage sites in Tamil Nadu today, reflecting the continuity of this ancient indigenous worship.

---

## 6. Tamil Language and Sangam Literature

The **Sangam** was an assembly of poets held under royal patronage in Madurai.

### **The Texts (Compiled c. AD 300-600)**

1. **Narrative (Melkannakku):** 18 Major Works (Heroic poetry glorifying wars/cattle raids). Reflects early megalithic/pastoral life.
2. **Didactic (Kilkanakku):** 18 Minor Works (Code of conduct). Written later by Brahmana scholars.

### **Key Works**

* **Tolkkappiyam:** Grammar and poetics.
* **Tirukkural:** Philosophy and wise maxims.
* **Epics (c. 6th Century AD):**
* **Silappadikaram:** Brightest gem; love story of Kovalan, Madhavi, and Kannagi.
* **Manimekalai:** Story of Kovalan and Madhavi's daughter.



> **Present Day Connection:**
> **Kannagi Statues:** The heroine of *Silappadikaram*, Kannagi, is worshipped as the epitome of chastity. Statues of her holding her anklet (the pivotal object in the story) stand in Chennai (Marina Beach) and in parts of Kerala and Sri Lanka.

### **Writing**

* Known before the Christian era.
* **Tamil-Brahmi Inscriptions:** Found in caves in Madurai (2nd-1st C. BC) and on potsherds. These mark the beginning of written Tamil history.

## 7. Chronology

| Year | Event |
| --- | --- |
| **1000 BC** | Beginning of Megalithic culture. |
| **4th C BC** | Cultural contact between North and Deep South (*Tamizhakam*). |
| **2nd C BC** | Chola king Elara conquers Sri Lanka; Megalithic people move to fertile basins. |
| **2nd-1st C BC** | Tamil-Brahmi inscriptions in Madurai caves. |
| **1st C AD** | Discovery of Monsoon; Roman trade booms. |
| **2nd C AD** | Chera power declines; **Senguttuvan** rules. |
| **AD 300-600** | Final composition of Sangam literature. |
| **6th C AD** | Composition of epics *Silappadikaram* and *Manimekalai*. |
| **9th C AD** | Cholas re-emerge as a major power. |
